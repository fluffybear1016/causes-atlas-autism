# HYP-0065 Heat shock response insufficiency 'fever effect' subtype


Curran 2007 documented that ~80% of autistic children showed behavioral improvement during fever. Subsequent animal work showed whole-body hyperthermia improves behavior in C58/J and Shank3B- mice. Hypothesis: a subset of autism reflects insufficient baseline heat shock response that is transiently rescued by HSF1 activation (fever, sauna, exercise, sulforaphane).


**Affected population:** Subset; ~80% by parental report (Curran 2007)


**Notes:** Curran 2007 Pediatrics; hyperthermia mouse models.
