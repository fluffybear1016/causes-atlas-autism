# HYP-0072 Epigenetic canalization failure


Autism arises when epigenetic programming systems (DNA methylation, histone modifications, chromatin structure) fail to canalize development into a typical trajectory at key neurodevelopmental loci. Methylation changes at promoters of RELN, GAD1, MECP2/EGR2, GABRB3, and other genes can shift inhibitory signaling and cortical maturation without any change to the underlying DNA sequence. Twin discordance studies (Wong 2014 monozygotic twins discordant for ASD) document dozens of differentially-methylated regions including at GABA receptor loci. Distinct from HYP-0003 (maternal folate deficiency, upstream nutritional input) in that the failure is in the epigenetic *programming* itself rather than in upstream methyl-donor supply. Implicates the methylation cycle (MEC-0003) but as an output mechanism, with the canalization failure being a higher-level systems property.


**Affected population:** Substantial subset (twin discordance studies suggest variable penetrance)


**Notes:** Epigenetic-systems hypothesis. Connects to MEC-0003 (impaired methylation) as one output mechanism; broader than that mechanism alone. Implicates GABRB3 promoter methylation specifically, linking to HYP-0071.
