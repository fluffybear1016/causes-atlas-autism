# 🧬 Hannah Poling Framework

The Hannah Poling framework is the **central organizing principle of this atlas**. It says: **autism causation is individual, not population-level**. For any specific child, what matters is not the average risk in the population, but the conditional risk *given that specific child's biology and exposures*.

## The formula

```
causation = susceptibility (P) × trigger (E) → mechanism (M) → phenotype (Φ)
```

Or in conditional-probability terms:

```
P(Φ | susceptibility, exposure) >> P(Φ | exposure alone)
```

For a child with a specific susceptibility profile (genetic, mitochondrial, immune, methylation, microbial) interacting with a specific exposure (infection, toxicant, vaccination, fever, metabolic stress) at a specific developmental window — the resulting risk of developing the autism phenotype is fundamentally different from the population average for that exposure.

## Where the name comes from

In 2008, the federal Vaccine Court ruled in **Poling v. Secretary of Health and Human Services** that vaccines administered to a 19-month-old child named Hannah Poling, who had pre-existing mitochondrial dysfunction, "significantly aggravated an underlying mitochondrial disorder, which predisposed her to deficits in cellular energy metabolism, and manifested as a regressive encephalopathy with features of autism spectrum disorder."

This is one of the few federally-adjudicated, legally-binding rulings on vaccine-injury causation. n=1, but it formally establishes the framework: **predisposing condition + triggering exposure → mechanism → autism-feature phenotype**.

The principle isn't limited to vaccines. It applies to any exposure that interacts with any susceptibility. Maternal infection during pregnancy. Heavy metal exposure. Glyphosate. Fever-induced regression. Surgery. Antibiotic-induced microbiome collapse. Each is a candidate "trigger" for a child whose specific "susceptibility" makes them vulnerable.

The atlas record for the federal ruling: [[SRC-001418 Hannah Poling federal Vaccine Court 2008 ruling]].

## Why this framework matters

### For mainstream public health

Mainstream public health calculates **population-average risk-benefit**. A vaccine, medication, or environmental exposure that causes harm in 1% of children but benefit in 99% gets recommended for everyone. The 1% is statistically invisible.

This framework is appropriate at the population level. It's profoundly inappropriate at the individual level for a parent making decisions for one specific susceptible child.

### For an individual family

Every autism intervention decision a parent makes is conditional. Should I vaccinate this specific child on this specific schedule? Should I try this specific intervention? Should I avoid this specific exposure?

The answer depends on the child's specific susceptibility profile, not on population averages.

## Naviaux's 3-Hit operationalization

Per [[Naviaux_Robert]] 2026 (published in *Autism Research*, atlas record [[SRC-001448 41902612]]):

**Hit 1 — Inherited genetic / epigenetic sensitization.** Variants in mitochondrial, calcium-handling, or purinergic-signaling genes that lower the threshold for entering the cell danger response (CDR) state and raise the threshold for exiting it.

**Hit 2 — Early CDR activation.** A prenatal or early-postnatal trigger that activates the CDR. Maternal infection, immune activation, metabolic disturbance, toxicant exposure.

**Hit 3 — Persistent or recurrent CDR triggers during the critical window.** 3-6 months of CDR activation occurring sometime between the late first trimester and 18-36 months of age. The window matters because that's when the relevant developmental state transitions are happening.

This is the clearest formalization of the Hannah Poling framework in published peer-reviewed literature. Susceptibility (Hit 1) × Trigger (Hit 2) × Window (Hit 3) = autism-feature phenotype.

## What this means for a parent

1. **Your child's autism is not "average autism."** It's a specific intersection of their susceptibility and exposures. Workup should map their specific profile.

2. **Population-average studies don't tell you about your child.** A study showing "no average effect" doesn't mean "no effect in your child" — it usually means effect heterogeneity that gets washed out at the population level. See [[CLAUDE]] §9.

3. **Stratification is everything.** Get the workup. Identify which susceptibility profile your child has. Match interventions to that profile. Avoid generic protocols.

4. **Mainstream pediatrics gives population averages; functional medicine operationalizes individual-level.** The functional medicine framework for autism (MAPS / [[Frye_Richard]] / [[Walsh_William]] / [[Hazan_Sabine]] / [[Bock_Kenneth]] lineages) is the practical clinical implementation of this individual-level framework.

5. **Prevention is also individual.** If you're planning a future pregnancy, the [[topics/omics/Epigenetics_of_Autism|preconception optimization framework]] for both parents directly applies the Hannah Poling framework's prevention arm.

## Bottom line

Autism is not one thing. It's many overlapping subsets of children, each with different susceptibility profiles, each potentially responsive to different interventions. The job of every parent and clinician is to identify *which subset this specific child is in* — not to treat autism generically.

The Hannah Poling framework is the formal name for that idea. It's the most important single concept in the atlas.
