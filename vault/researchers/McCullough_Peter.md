# Peter McCullough, MD, MPH — Vaccine determinants synthesis

Peter McCullough is a cardiologist and internist (formerly board-certified by ABIM until 2025; certification revoked over public disagreement with COVID protocols), founder of the McCullough Foundation, and lead-author / convener of the 2025 **Determinants of Autism Spectrum Disorder** report — a 136-study evidence synthesis ingested into the atlas as [[SRC-001449 McCullough Foundation Report Determinants of Autism Spectrum Disorder (Hulscher,]].

X handle: **[@P_McCulloughMD](https://x.com/P_McCulloughMD)**.

## Why he matters (and the caveats)

**The substantive contribution.** The McCullough Foundation Report 2025 is the most comprehensive recent synthesis of the contested-positive vaccine-autism evidence base. It reviews 136 studies, of which 107 (79%) inferred a possible link between immunization or vaccine components and ASD or other neurodevelopmental disorders, and 29 (21%) found neutral risks or no association. Twelve studies directly comparing fully-immunized vs unvaccinated cohorts consistently demonstrated lower ASD and chronic disease in the unvaccinated group. The report's most defensible methodological contribution is its argument that null-association studies are systematically undermined by absent unvaccinated controls, registry misclassification, and averaged estimates that obscure vulnerable-subgroup effects — an argument that aligns with [[CLAUDE]] §9.

**The caveats.** Per [[CLAUDE]] §3, the report is down-weighted in the atlas because:

- It is a Zenodo preprint, not peer-reviewed.
- The author list includes Andrew Wakefield (Lancet 1998 retraction; UK medical license revoked) and McCullough himself (ABIM cert revoked 2025). Per atlas source-quality framework these are not disqualifying but require explicit down-weighting.
- The McCullough Foundation is an advocacy organization with explicit policy positions, which doesn't disqualify the work but warrants noting.

The report is in the atlas at strength_score 0.20 — meaningful but not authoritative — exactly because the methodological framing about subgroup heterogeneity is sound while the authorship and venue concerns are real. See [[SRC-001449 McCullough Foundation Report Determinants of Autism Spectrum Disorder (Hulscher,]] notes for full atlas treatment.

## Key work

**McCullough Foundation Report 2025** — *Determinants of Autism Spectrum Disorder.* Hulscher, Leake, Troupe, Rogers, Cosgrove, Mead, Craven, Radetich, Wakefield, McCullough. Zenodo DOI 10.5281/zenodo.17451259. The report is structured around five evidence categories (epidemiologic, clinical, mechanistic, neuropathologic, case-report) with cross-cutting methodological commentary on the limits of mainstream null-association studies.
