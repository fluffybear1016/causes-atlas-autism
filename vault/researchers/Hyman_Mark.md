# Mark Hyman, MD — Functional medicine generalist

Mark Hyman is a board-certified family physician and the founder of the Cleveland Clinic Center for Functional Medicine — the most prestigious institutional adoption of the functional-medicine model in mainstream US healthcare. His autism contribution is more cultural / educational than primary-research: he is the most-recognized public-facing voice translating the functional-medicine framework (gut health, mitochondrial support, nutrient sufficiency, methylation, environmental detoxification, reduction of inflammatory dietary load) into terms a mainstream audience can act on.

## Why he matters

Functional medicine for autism is rigorous biochemistry implemented as personalized clinical care ([[Frye_Richard]], [[Walsh_William]], [[Bock_Kenneth]], [[Yasko_Amy]], [[Hazan_Sabine]] all operate within this frame). Hyman has done as much as anyone to bring this framework into mainstream medical institutions and parent awareness. The Cleveland Clinic Center for Functional Medicine is the highest-prestige institutional implementation of the framework in US healthcare.

He is not the deepest autism specialist. He is the broadest functional-medicine generalist, and his framework is highly compatible with the more autism-specific work of the others in this folder.

## Concept summary

**Food as medicine.** Hyman's central frame: most of what mainstream medicine treats as separate chronic-disease categories share root causes in dysregulated nutrition, inflammation, gut dysfunction, environmental toxicant load, and metabolic dysfunction. Autism is one manifestation; ADHD, allergies, autoimmune disease, and adult chronic disease are others.

**Personalized over standardized.** Functional medicine starts with the individual patient's biochemistry, history, environment, and genetics — and customizes intervention accordingly. This is the [[Hannah Poling framework]] applied to general health.

**Food, sleep, movement, stress, environment, relationships.** Hyman's framework starts with the lifestyle layer before pharmacology. For autism specifically, this means: clean diet (often gluten-free / casein-free trial, see [[INT (GFCF) — pending Session 3.5B]]), sleep regulation, sensory environment, connection.
