# Autism Researchers — Elite Map (2023–2026)

This is the human layer of the atlas. Hypotheses, mechanisms, and interventions don't appear from nowhere — specific researchers spent decades building the evidence base, often against significant institutional pushback. This folder is the "who's actually doing the work" map for a parent or family member trying to understand autism at the level of someone who treats it.

The atlas itself (see [[00_INDEX]]) is the deterministic causal-graph + scoring layer. This folder is the "follow these people, read these papers, watch these accounts" layer that feeds into it.

## How to use this

If you're new and overwhelmed: **start with [[Hazan_Sabine]]** and **[[Frye_Richard]]**. They cover two different but synergistic intervention paths (gut microbiome and folate metabolism) that account for the largest and most replicated subgroup-specific effect sizes in the field. Both have testable, biomarker-driven protocols. Both treat autism as something with mechanistic causes you can intervene on, not just a behavioral diagnosis.

If you want the unifying theory: read [[Naviaux_Robert]] (the 3-Hit Cell Danger Response model — already ingested into the atlas as [[SRC-001448 41902612]]) and [[Herbert_Martha]] (the whole-body autism framework that prefigured much of Naviaux's synthesis).

If you want comprehensive parent-facing books: read [[Bock_Kenneth]]'s *Healing the New Childhood Epidemics*, [[Herbert_Martha]]'s *The Autism Revolution*, [[Frye_Richard]]'s *The Folate Fix*, and [[Walsh_William]]'s *Nutrient Power*.

## Tiered organization

The tiers are the same scoring tiers used elsewhere in the atlas — they reflect *source-quality weighting*, not personal endorsement. A researcher in Tier 2 (contested) is not "wrong"; they're operating in a domain where mainstream consensus and primary evidence diverge, and the atlas weights mainstream consensus as one input among several rather than authoritative (per [[CLAUDE]] §1).

Legal / policy actors (RFK Jr. at HHS, Aaron Siri's litigation infrastructure, Mary Holland at CHD) are tracked through their primary-document SRC entries in the atlas (see [[SRC-001418 Hannah Poling federal Vaccine Court 2008 ruling]] and [[SRC-001420 ACIP September 2025 Hep B birth-dose review]]) rather than as researcher pages here, since this folder is for people doing primary scientific or clinical work on autism causation and treatment.

## Cross-cutting themes (2023–2026)

Five threads tie this entire group together. Each one corresponds to a specific atlas hypothesis or mechanism.

**1. Subgroup-specific biomarkers enable targeted intervention.** The single most important conceptual shift in autism research in the past five years: stop treating autism as one thing. FRAA-positive kids respond to leucovorin ([[Frye_Richard]]). Bifidobacterium-depleted kids respond to FMT ([[Hazan_Sabine]], [[Adams_James]]). Mitochondrial-vulnerable kids respond to mito support and avoidance of CDR triggers ([[Rossignol_Daniel]]). Methylation-impaired kids respond to methyl donors with biotype matching ([[James_Jill]], [[Walsh_William]], [[Yasko_Amy]]). PANS/PANDAS-positive kids respond to immune therapy ([[Swedo_Sue]], [[Cunningham_Madeleine]]). Chloride-imbalanced kids respond to bumetanide ([[BenAri_Yehezkel]]). PIN1-disrupted kids may respond to glyphosate avoidance ([[Seneff_Stephanie]]). None of these are universal cures. All of them have measurable biomarkers that predict response. The atlas operationalizes this as the [[CLAUDE]] §9 principle: *mixed published results are almost always effect heterogeneity, not absence of effect.*

**2. Multifactorial causation with environmental hits on vulnerable genetics during critical windows.** [[Naviaux_Robert]]'s 3-Hit model unifies what [[Frye_Richard]], [[Hazan_Sabine]], [[Rossignol_Daniel]], [[Theoharides_Theoharis]], [[Hooker_Brian]], [[Seneff_Stephanie]], [[Casanova_Manuel]], [[BenAri_Yehezkel]], and [[McCullough_Peter]] each see from their own angle. [[Herbert_Martha]]'s *The Autism Revolution* prefigured much of this synthesis years earlier. See [[SRC-001448 41902612]] for the formal framework. See [[HYP-0073 Developmental timing state-transition disorder]] for the developmental-window dimension.

**3. Microbiome as both biomarker and intervention target.** The convergence of [[Hazan_Sabine]]'s familial FMT cases, [[Adams_James]]'s MTT trials, [[Shaw_William]]'s organic acids panel work, and the broader gut-brain-axis literature points to a real, modifiable causal pathway in a substantial subgroup. See [[INT-0076 Fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT)]], [[INT-0025 Probiotics (multi-strain)]], [[PHE-0004 Autism + GI microbiome dysbiosis]].

**4. Methylation / one-carbon metabolism as the unifying biochemistry.** [[James_Jill]]'s biochemistry work + [[Frye_Richard]]'s clinical translation + [[Walsh_William]]'s biotyping + [[Yasko_Amy]]'s SNP-targeted protocols collectively establish that one-carbon metabolism (folate ↔ methionine ↔ transsulfuration cycles) is dysregulated in a major subgroup of autism, that the dysregulation is measurable, and that the underlying biology is correctable. See [[INT-0001 Leucovorin (folinic acid)]] (atlas calibration anchor at csrs 83.20).

**5. Regulatory-capture critique of mainstream consensus.** The Tier 2 researchers converge on the same structural argument: the institutions that produce the autism-prevention guidance have systematic conflicts of interest that distort what gets studied, what gets published, and what gets retracted. [[Hazan_Sabine]]'s "I won't publish my protocol because of the retraction machine" is the same critique [[Rogers_Toby]] makes about the FDA, [[Hooker_Brian]] makes about CDC MMR data, [[Thomas_Paul]] experienced firsthand, [[Shaw_Christopher]] has navigated for years, and [[McCullough_Peter]] makes systematically. The atlas takes this seriously per [[CLAUDE]] §1 and §3.
