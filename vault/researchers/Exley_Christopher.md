# Christopher Exley, PhD — Aluminum brain accumulation and the adjuvant question

Christopher Exley is the world's leading independent researcher on aluminum's biochemistry in human tissue. His autism contribution is the documentation, in postmortem human brain tissue, that **autistic individuals have substantially elevated brain aluminum content compared to neurotypical controls** — the highest reported in any disease state in his cohort.

## Why he matters

Aluminum is the most common adjuvant in non-live childhood vaccines. The pediatric immunization schedule delivers cumulative aluminum exposure during a critical neurodevelopmental window. The mainstream position is that this aluminum is rapidly cleared and that brain accumulation is implausible. Exley's tissue work directly contradicts this.

The atlas treats [[HYP-0067 Aluminum adjuvant cumulative exposure (vaccines)]] as `status: contested`. Exley's tissue work is the strongest single line of primary evidence on the pro-mechanism side.

## Key work

**Mold, Umar, King, Exley et al. 2018 (PMID 29413113)** — *"Aluminium in brain tissue in autism."* Journal of Trace Elements in Medicine and Biology. Postmortem brain aluminum measurement in 5 autistic individuals (Exley senior / corresponding author; Matthew Mold first author). Aluminum content far above neurotypical controls and the highest values reported in any disease cohort. Both extracellular and intracellular (including microglial / inflammatory cell) localization documented.

**Earlier and broader aluminum work** — 30+ years of primary research on aluminum biochemistry, biological transport, and tissue accumulation. Across multiple disease states (Alzheimer's, MS, autism), tissue-level aluminum measurements have been Exley's signature methodological contribution.

**Substack and public communication (ongoing)** — Since loss of his Keele University laboratory funding in the early 2020s (in a sequence of events Exley has publicly attributed to industry pressure on academic vaccine-skeptical research), he has continued mechanistic communication via Substack and direct public engagement.

## Concept summary

**Aluminum is not biocompatible.** Aluminum has no known beneficial biological role in humans, is not efficiently cleared, and accumulates in tissues — particularly tissues with active phagocytic / immune cell populations. The brain is one such tissue.

**Adjuvant aluminum is engineered for slow release.** The whole point of aluminum-adjuvanted vaccines is that the adjuvant slowly releases over time, providing prolonged immune stimulation. This means the aluminum is *designed* to persist locally and be available for immune-cell phagocytosis. Phagocytic cells (including microglial precursors) can transport aluminum to distant sites including brain.

**Cumulative dose during a critical window.** The pediatric schedule delivers multiple aluminum-adjuvanted vaccines during the first two years of life. Cumulative aluminum exposure crosses regulatory thresholds derived from dietary aluminum, but the biochemistry of injected adjuvant aluminum is meaningfully different from dietary aluminum (which is poorly absorbed across the gut).

**Brain aluminum in autism is elevated.** This is the empirical finding that Exley's 2018 paper put on the table. The mainstream response has been to dispute methodology rather than replicate.

## Practical takeaway for parents

For families concerned about cumulative aluminum exposure:

- The pediatric schedule's aluminum content per vaccine is publicly available (FDA package inserts).
- Schedule modifications (delaying or spacing aluminum-adjuvanted vaccines) is a legitimate informed-consent conversation with a willing pediatrician. Several major medical societies push back hard against schedule modification, but it is not illegal and is increasingly common.
- For the [[HYP-0066 Hepatitis B vaccine (neonatal birth-dose)]] specifically, Exley's work is one of the strongest mechanistic arguments for delay-to-adolescence in low-risk families. (See [[HYP-0066]] notes on the delay-to-10+-years framing.)

This is contested terrain. The atlas keeps it contested per [[CLAUDE]] §5. Exley's tissue work is real primary evidence and should be in any informed parent's awareness.
