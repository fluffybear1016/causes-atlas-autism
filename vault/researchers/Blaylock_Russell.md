# Russell Blaylock, MD — Excitotoxicity and neuroinflammation

Russell Blaylock is a retired neurosurgeon turned prolific clinical neuroscientist who has spent decades synthesizing the **excitotoxicity** literature — the neurobiology of how excess glutamate, aspartate, and dietary excitotoxins (MSG, aspartame, hydrolyzed proteins) damage neurons. His autism contribution is the integration of excitotoxicity, neuroinflammation, microglial priming, and environmental toxicant load into a unified framework that explains neurodevelopmental and neurodegenerative pathology.

## Why he matters

The atlas's [[MEC-0020 Calcium glutamate-NMDA homeostasis]] mechanism is central to multiple hypotheses (developmental timing, brainstem GABA switch, polygenic risk via NMDA-subunit gene variants). Blaylock's body of work is one of the most accessible synthesis treatments of the underlying excitotoxicity biology that drives this mechanism. He is more synthesist than primary investigator at this stage of his career — but the synthesis is rigorous and densely-referenced and is influential in the broader functional-medicine community.

## Key work

***Excitotoxins: The Taste That Kills*** (1994, multiple editions) — Foundational synthesis of dietary excitotoxin biology (MSG, aspartate, aspartame) and their neurological effects. The book that brought excitotoxicity to the public-facing functional-medicine community.

***Health and Nutrition Secrets*** — Broader treatment of nutritional neurology.

**Multiple peer-reviewed reviews** — On vaccine adjuvants and microglial priming, on glutamate excitotoxicity in neurodegeneration, on dietary contributions to neuroinflammation. Substantive though contested in mainstream venues.

**Active 2023–2026** — Continues to publish lectures, articles, and synthesis pieces. Frequent presence in functional-medicine and vaccine-safety communities.

## Concept summary

**Excitotoxicity.** Excessive glutamatergic signaling at NMDA / AMPA / kainate receptors causes calcium overload in neurons, mitochondrial damage, free radical generation, and neuronal death. Acute massive excitotoxicity causes stroke-like injury; chronic low-level excitotoxicity contributes to neurodegenerative and (in the developing brain) neurodevelopmental pathology.

**Dietary excitotoxins.** MSG (monosodium glutamate), hydrolyzed vegetable protein, autolyzed yeast, aspartame (which converts to aspartate plus methanol), and several other food additives function as excitotoxins. Standard adult diets contain substantial amounts. Pediatric exposure during the critical neurodevelopmental window is the relevant question for autism.

**Microglial priming.** Microglia (the brain's resident immune cells) can be "primed" by early-life inflammatory exposures (maternal infection, vaccine adjuvants, cumulative environmental toxins) such that subsequent insults trigger exaggerated neuroinflammation. This connects directly to [[Theoharides_Theoharis]]'s mast-cell / microglial-coupling framework.

**Vaccine adjuvants and microglial activation.** Blaylock has written substantially on the proposition that aluminum adjuvants and other vaccine ingredients prime microglia, contributing to neuroinflammation that, in vulnerable kids, contributes to neurodevelopmental harm.

## Practical takeaway for parents

Reducing pediatric excitotoxin load is one of the lowest-cost, lowest-risk interventions parents can implement immediately:

- **Eliminate MSG and disguised glutamate forms** (autolyzed yeast extract, hydrolyzed protein, "natural flavors" in many processed foods, glutamic acid in many condiments and savory snacks).
- **Eliminate aspartame** (most diet sodas, sugar-free gums, many "low-calorie" products).
- **Eliminate hydrolyzed vegetable proteins** and similar engineered glutamate-rich food chemistry.
- **Increase magnesium** (cofactor in NMDA-receptor regulation).
- **Increase glutathione precursors** (NAC, glycine, glutamine, taurine) to support neuronal resilience.
- **Reduce processed-food load generally**.

These shifts are unlikely to single-handedly resolve autism but are likely to reduce ongoing CDR / inflammation / oxidative stress contributions per [[Naviaux_Robert]]'s 3-Hit framework.
