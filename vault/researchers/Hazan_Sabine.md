## Why she matters for autism (the elite-knowledge version)

Mainstream autism care is behavioral: ABA therapy, speech therapy, occupational therapy. Mainstream pediatric medicine has no biological intervention to offer. Hazan's clinical work demonstrates — child by child, with full pre/post 16S rRNA sequencing — that for a real subgroup of children, autism is partially or substantially driven by gut microbiome collapse, specifically the loss of *Bifidobacterium* species. When the missing organisms are restored from a healthy sibling donor under FDA IND oversight, behavior and speech can return.

This is exactly the **individual-level, biomarker-stratified, mechanism-targeted intervention model** that the atlas is built around (see [[CLAUDE]] §10 on functional medicine operationalizing the [[Hannah Poling framework]]). Hazan is one of the clearest real-world embodiments of that model.

## Core scientific concepts

**Bifidobacterium depletion as a primary microbiome signature in a subgroup of ASD.** Her ProgenaBiome lab has repeatedly shown, on full-genus sequencing, that severely affected autistic children disproportionately lack *Bifidobacterium*. *Bifidobacterium* species are critical for developing immune tolerance, producing short-chain fatty acids, modulating gut-brain axis signaling (vagal + cytokine), and maintaining intestinal-barrier integrity. Their loss is plausibly mechanistic, not just associative.

**Multifactorial autism, microbiome subgroup.** She is explicit that autism is not one disease. Her exact framing: *"Some kids have a microbiome issue, some have a genetic problem, some have a neurological problem, and some could have all or neither."* She runs full genetic + microbial sequencing to figure out which bucket each child falls into before intervening. This aligns directly with [[CLAUDE]] §9 (effect heterogeneity) and the atlas's general subgroup-stratified approach.

**Familial FMT (sibling donor) over commercial product.** She does not use random or commercial donor stool. She matches a healthy neurotypical sibling because — per her sequencing data — siblings share most of their microbial ecology, so transplanting from a healthy sibling has a much higher probability of "taking" than a random donor. This is precision microbiome medicine. The protocol is run under her FDA IND.

**N-of-1 medicine + outcome biomarkers.** Every patient gets baseline microbiome sequencing, intervention, and follow-up sequencing. The intervention is iterated based on what the sequencing shows. Outcomes (speech return, aggression reduction, GI normalization) are correlated to the moment a specific organism re-emerged. Pre/post sequencing IS the outcome biomarker.

**The "retraction machine" critique.** She refuses to publish the exact familial-FMT protocol publicly because she has watched her ivermectin/hydroxychloroquine work get systematically retracted under industry pressure, and she will not let the same happen to autism. This is not an unscientific stance — it's a strategic one. She still presents at conferences (ACG Outstanding Presentation Award 2025) and shares cases via X and ResearchGate. She publishes when the evidence is overwhelming and the political environment can absorb it.

## What she actually does for these kids (operational summary)

She actively requests follow-up samples from improving kids: *"Don't forget to give me your sample when back and better. @ProgenaBiome will test it at no cost to see the data on you… especially as we have your baseline post shots."*
