# Dr. Adi Aran

**Affiliation:** Director, Neuropediatric Unit, Shaare Zedek Medical Center, Jerusalem, Israel

## Why this researcher matters

Dr. Adi Aran ran the **first major worldwide clinical trial of cannabinoid therapy for autism** — a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled study of CBD-rich cannabis in children with autism and severe behavioral problems. His work established the modern endocannabinoid framework for autism behavioral intervention and is the empirical basis for clinical CBD use globally.

## Key contributions

### Cannabinoid trials in autism (2018–present)

Aran's group at Shaare Zedek pioneered the use of **CBD-rich cannabis (20:1 CBD:THC ratio)** in children and young adults (ages 5–29) with autism + severe behavioral problems:

- **2018 retrospective feasibility study** ([PMID 30382443](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30382443/)) — N=60 children, retrospective; 80% of parents reported behavioral improvement
- **2021 RCT** ([PMID 33536055](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/33536055/)) — randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled, crossover trial of cannabinoid treatment for autism; ~50% of children showed improved disruptive behavior + sleep
- **Subsequent CSHQ sleep parameter analysis** — cannabinoid treatment also improved sleep architecture

The Aran trials are the **methodologically rigorous reference point** for endocannabinoid intervention in autism. They establish:
- Tolerability of pediatric CBD-rich cannabis at clinically relevant doses
- Modest but real behavioral improvement in a responder subset
- The case for endocannabinoid system involvement in autism phenotype regulation

### Endocannabinoid framework for autism

The biological basis for Aran's work parallels the 2-AG / anandamide work in [[PHE-0006 Fragile X (FMR1)|Fragile X]] and the GABAergic modulation in [[PHE-0007 GABA_Cl- imbalance phenotype|GABA/Cl- imbalance]]. CBD modulates GABA-A allosterically and influences microglial activation — making it a candidate cross-phenotype intervention.

### "Neuroprotective cocktail" follow-up trial

Aran's group has been advancing a follow-up trial of a multi-component "neuroprotective cocktail" combining endocannabinoid support with other components. As of late 2023 reports, this was preparing for Phase II.
