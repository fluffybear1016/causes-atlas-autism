# Geraldine Dawson, PhD — Duke autism research lead

Geraldine Dawson is the Director of the **Duke Center for Autism and Brain Development** and the autism-research lead for the Duke pediatric stem cell program. With [[Kurtzberg_Joanne]] (cell therapy lead at the Marcus Center for Cellular Cures), she has co-led the most rigorous peer-reviewed clinical trials of cord blood for autism — the Phase 1 ([[SRC-001450 28378499]]) and Phase 2 RCT ([[SRC-001451 32444220]]) that constitute the gold-standard evidence base for cell therapy in autism.

## Why she matters

Dawson's contribution spans two distinct domains:

1. **Cord blood autism trials.** Her partnership with Kurtzberg established that autologous cord blood is safe in young autistic children and produces measurable behavioral signal in stratified subgroups (non-anxious, normal-IQ, inflammatory-marker-positive). The Phase 2 RCT subgroup analysis is the methodologically defensible signal that mainstream interpretations dismissed as "negative."

2. **Early intervention research.** Dawson is also one of the foundational figures in autism early-intervention research, particularly the **Early Start Denver Model (ESDM)** — a behavioral early-intervention approach with substantial peer-reviewed evidence base for very young autistic children. ESDM is mainstream-credible and complements (rather than competes with) biomedical interventions.

## Key work

**[[SRC-001450 28378499]] — Dawson, Sun, Davlantis, … Kurtzberg 2017 Stem Cells Translational Medicine**
- Phase 1 autologous cord blood for autism. 25 children age 2-6, single-center open-label.
- Established safety profile + behavioral signal informing Phase 2 design.

**[[SRC-001451 32444220]] — Dawson, Sun, Baker, … Kurtzberg 2020 Journal of Pediatrics**
- Phase 2 RCT autologous cord blood vs placebo. 180 children age 2-7. 6-month primary outcome.
- Negative on primary outcome in unstratified analysis. **Critical subgroup signal:** non-anxious / normal-IQ / inflammatory-positive children showed significant improvement vs placebo.
- The methodologically defensible signal in this trial validates the [[CLAUDE]] §9 effect-heterogeneity principle.

**Early Start Denver Model (ESDM)** — Dawson's foundational early-intervention work. Multiple peer-reviewed RCTs demonstrate ESDM's behavioral benefit when delivered intensively in the very-early-intervention window (12-30 months).

**Dawson's broader autism research portfolio** — biomarker development, eye-tracking, EEG-based phenotyping. Many of these biomarker tools feed into the responder-stratification analyses that make the cord blood trials interpretable.

**Active 2023-2026** — continuing trials, expanding cell therapy indications, ongoing early-intervention research.

## Practical takeaway for parents

For families considering cell therapy: Dawson and Kurtzberg's program is the gold-standard pathway. The trials are research-funded (typically free to participants), the protocols are FDA-IND-overseen, and the outcome assessment is multi-modality (behavioral + EEG + eye-tracking + biomarker).

Trial enrollment information: ClinicalTrials.gov → "Duke autism cord blood" — also accessible through Duke Center for Autism and Brain Development directly.

For families looking at non-cell-therapy options: Dawson's ESDM work is one of the most rigorously-evidenced behavioral early-intervention frameworks. ESDM-trained clinicians work alongside biomedical interventions, not against them.
