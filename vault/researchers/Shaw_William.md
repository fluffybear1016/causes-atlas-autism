# William Shaw, PhD — The organic acids panel and clinical biochemistry of autism

William Shaw is the clinical biochemist who built **Great Plains Laboratory** (now **Mosaic Diagnostics**) and developed and refined the **Organic Acids Test (OAT)** — the urinary metabolite panel that has become one of the most-used clinical biomarker tests in functional/integrative autism medicine. The OAT is essentially a chemical fingerprint of gut microbial metabolism + mitochondrial function + neurotransmitter metabolism + detoxification capacity, all from a single urine sample. It is one of the practical foundations of biomarker-driven autism workup.

## Why he matters

Most clinical autism workup is qualitative (behavior rating scales, observation, history) rather than quantitative (lab measurements of biochemistry). Shaw's OAT and the broader Mosaic Diagnostics test menu (genetic SNPs, mycotoxins, heavy metals, glyphosate, organic acids, amino acids, gut microbiome) are the operational toolkit that lets a functional-medicine clinician actually *measure* what's happening biochemically and target intervention accordingly. The atlas's [[Session 3.5A]] biomarker schema design will substantially incorporate the OAT and adjacent Mosaic tests as core biomarker entries.

## Key work

**Discovery of arabinose / yeast metabolite signature in autism (1990s)** — Shaw was among the first to document that autistic children have elevated urinary arabinose and other yeast/fungal metabolites, suggesting overgrowth of *Candida* and related opportunistic fungi in the gut. This finding launched the antifungal-protocol arm of biomedical autism intervention.

**HPHPA (3-(3-hydroxyphenyl)-3-hydroxypropionic acid)** — Shaw identified this as a specific *Clostridia* metabolite, and demonstrated elevated HPHPA in autistic children correlates with behavioral severity. HPHPA inhibits dopamine beta-hydroxylase, distorting dopamine/norepinephrine balance — providing a direct biochemical mechanism linking gut dysbiosis to neurobehavioral phenotype.

**4-cresol and other clostridial metabolites** — Subsequent work documenting additional gut-bacterial metabolites with neuroactive properties.

**Multiple peer-reviewed papers + book chapters** — On organic acids, fungal overgrowth, mitochondrial markers, and clinical interpretation of metabolite panels.

***Biological Treatments for Autism and PDD***. Shaw's foundational clinical book.

**Continuing through 2023–2026** — Mosaic Diagnostics (rebrand from Great Plains Lab) continues to expand the test menu. Shaw remains active in clinical-biochemistry education and conference presentations.

## Concept summary

**Organic acids panel (OAT) as broad screen.** A single urine sample tested for ~75 organic acid metabolites covers: yeast/fungal overgrowth (arabinose, citramalic acid, tartaric acid), bacterial overgrowth (HPHPA, 4-cresol, indican), mitochondrial function (citric acid cycle metabolites — citrate, isocitrate, succinate, fumarate, malate), fatty acid oxidation, neurotransmitter metabolism (HVA, VMA, 5-HIAA, quinolinic acid), and oxidative stress markers. This is one of the highest information-density single tests in clinical biochemistry.

**Gut metabolites → brain function.** The OAT operationalizes the gut-brain axis at the level of measurable urinary metabolites. Gut dysbiosis produces specific neuroactive compounds. Those compounds reach the brain. They distort neurotransmitter biology. The OAT shows what's actually happening in that pipeline.

**Treatable findings.** Elevated arabinose → antifungal protocol (nystatin, caprylic acid, undecylenic acid, biofilm disruptors) + Bifidobacterium-supportive nutrition. Elevated HPHPA → targeted *Clostridia* treatment (specific antibiotics, biofilm disruptors, probiotic recolonization). Mitochondrial cycle abnormalities → mitochondrial cofactor support per [[Rossignol_Daniel]].

## Practical takeaway for parents

The Organic Acids Test (OAT) from Mosaic Diagnostics (mosaicdx.com) is one of the highest-yield single tests in initial autism biochemistry workup. It is widely available through functional-medicine practitioners and DirectLabs / DirectAccess in some states. Cost is typically $300–400 self-pay. Result interpretation requires a knowledgeable clinician — the panel's information density is precisely what makes interpretation matter.

Mosaic also offers a **GPL-TOX** mycotoxin / chemical exposure panel, **OAT** + organic acids, **MycoTOX** (mold exposure), **glyphosate**, and several other commercially-available biomarker tests. Together these constitute the most comprehensive functional-medicine biochemistry panel available for autism workup.
