# Prof. Isabel Dziobek

**Affiliation:** Professor, Head of Working Group "Clinical Psychology of Social Interaction," Humboldt-Universität zu Berlin; Principal Investigator, NeuroCure Cluster of Excellence, Charité — Universitätsmedizin Berlin

## Why this researcher matters

Dziobek leads Germany's most translational social-cognition research program in autism. Her group develops **psychometric tools** for assessing autism social-cognition deficits in adults (the Movie for the Assessment of Social Cognition, MASC) and runs **psychotherapy trials** specifically adapted for adults with autism. Her work fills a gap that US autism research has historically underserved — the adult autism population.

## Key contributions

### MASC — Movie for the Assessment of Social Cognition

Dziobek developed the MASC, a video-based assessment of theory-of-mind + social inference in autism. It:
- Uses naturalistic dynamic social scenes
- Probes mental state attribution in real time
- Discriminates autism from non-autism populations more sensitively than static-image theory-of-mind tasks
- Is now widely used in European autism research

### Adult autism — clinical phenotyping + psychotherapy

Dziobek's group has been central in establishing adult autism diagnostic services in Germany:
- Differential diagnosis from personality disorders (often misdiagnosed as borderline or narcissistic)
- Co-occurring depression, anxiety in adult autism
- **Adapted CBT** for autism — cognitive-behavioral therapy modified for autistic cognition styles
- Group-based social cognition training programs

### Functional MRI of social cognition

Her NeuroCure-affiliated work uses fMRI to map social-cognition networks in autism — identifying default mode network + mentalizing-network differences that connect to [[BIO-0140 Resting-state fMRI default mode network connectivity|biomarker fMRI signatures]] across the lifespan.
