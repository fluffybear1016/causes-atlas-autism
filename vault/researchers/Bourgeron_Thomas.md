# Prof. Thomas Bourgeron

**Affiliation:** Professor, Institut Pasteur, Paris; Head, Human Genetics and Cognitive Functions Unit; Université Paris Cité

## Why this researcher matters

Bourgeron is the architect of the **synaptic genes framework** for autism — the unifying theoretical structure that organizes hundreds of autism risk genes into a coherent biological story about synapse function. His 2003 discovery of NLGN3/NLGN4 mutations in autism families was the **first identification of a single-gene autism etiology in non-syndromic autism** and triggered the modern wave of autism gene discovery. His 2015 review in *Nature Reviews Neuroscience* is the canonical synthesis of autism synaptic biology.

## Key contributions

### Discovery of NLGN3/NLGN4 mutations (2003)

Bourgeron's team identified mutations in **neuroligin 3 (NLGN3) and neuroligin 4 (NLGN4)** in two Swedish families with autism — published in *Nature Genetics* 2003. This was a foundational moment because:

- It identified the **first non-syndromic autism gene** with rare high-penetrance mutations
- The genes encode **synaptic adhesion molecules** — focusing the field on synapse biology
- It validated the strategy of studying **rare large-effect mutations** in autism families

### SHANK3 + SHANK family work

Bourgeron's group identified **SHANK3 mutations as a cause of autism with intellectual disability** (Phelan-McDermid syndrome region). SHANK3 is a postsynaptic scaffolding protein. SHANK3 mutations disrupt postsynaptic density assembly — providing direct mechanistic evidence for the synaptic-protein-synthesis convergent framework that also characterizes [[PHE-0005 mTOR pathway syndromic (TSC, PTEN)]] and [[PHE-0006 Fragile X (FMR1)]].

### Synaptic gene framework synthesis (2015)

Bourgeron's 2015 *Nature Reviews Neuroscience* review consolidates the field: autism risk genes converge on **synaptic structure, transmission, and plasticity** — encompassing:
- Postsynaptic scaffolding (SHANK1/2/3, PSD-95, GKAP)
- Synaptic adhesion (NLGN1-4, NRXN1-3)
- Translation regulation (FMRP, mTOR pathway, eIF4E)
- Chromatin remodeling (CHD8, MECP2, ARID1B)
- Voltage-gated channels (SCN2A, CACNA1C)

This synthesis is the empirical justification for organizing the atlas around mechanism-clusters that cross specific gene boundaries.

### EU-AIMS + LEAP cohort

Bourgeron has led European autism research consortia, including contributions to the EU-AIMS LEAP (Longitudinal European Autism Project) cohort — the largest multi-modal autism cohort in Europe.
