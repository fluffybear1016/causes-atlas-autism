# Eric Hollander, MD — Autism psychopharmacology and novel-target trials

Eric Hollander runs the Autism and Obsessive Compulsive Spectrum Program at Montefiore Medical Center / Albert Einstein College of Medicine. His autism contribution is the systematic clinical-trial investigation of **novel pharmacological targets** beyond the standard SSRI / antipsychotic toolkit — particularly **oxytocin, IGF-1, and cannabidiol (CBD)** in autism core symptoms.

## Why he matters

Mainstream autism psychopharmacology is limited: SSRIs for anxiety/OCD features, atypical antipsychotics (risperidone, aripiprazole) for irritability — these target comorbidities, not core autism symptoms. Hollander has spent decades building the trial infrastructure to test mechanism-targeted compounds against core social-communication deficits.

The atlas's [[Session 3.5B]] functional-medicine intervention expansion will add several Hollander-investigated agents (oxytocin, CBD) as formal interventions, with Hollander's RCTs as the primary peer-reviewed evidence basis.

## Key work

**Oxytocin in autism RCTs** — Multiple trials testing intranasal oxytocin in autism core symptoms (social motivation, eye contact, repetitive behaviors). Mixed results consistent with effect heterogeneity across baseline oxytocin/vasopressin variant subgroups. Hollander's stratified analyses identified responder profiles that an unstratified study would miss.

**IGF-1 in Phelan-McDermid syndrome / SHANK3 deficiency** — Phase 2 trial of IGF-1 in autism with SHANK3 mutations. This is the cleanest example of genotype-stratified targeted intervention in autism trials: SHANK3 loss-of-function → synaptic protein deficit → IGF-1 rescues synaptic function in animal models → clinical trial in genotype-positive children. Subgroup-driven from the start.

**Cannabidiol (CBD) trials** — Multiple ongoing and recently-completed CBD trials in autism for irritability, anxiety, and sleep. Building on the broader epilepsy CBD evidence base ([[INT (rapamycin / CBD pending)]]).

**Active research portfolio (2023–2026)** — Continuing trials in oxytocin, CBD, and other novel targets.

## Concept summary

**Genotype-stratified pharmacology.** Hollander's IGF-1 / SHANK3 work is methodologically aligned with the [[Hannah Poling framework]]: stratify by biomarker (in this case genotype), intervene on the specific defect, document subgroup-specific response.

**Novel mechanism targets.** Oxytocin (social-cognition), IGF-1 (synaptic protein synthesis), CBD (CB1/CB2/multiple targets), and other emerging compounds — each with distinct mechanism rationale rather than generic neurotransmitter modulation.
