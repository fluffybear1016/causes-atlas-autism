# Brian Hooker, PhD — MMR reanalysis and the regressive-autism subgroup

Brian Hooker is a bioengineer and Senior Director of Science and Research at Children's Health Defense. His most influential work is the **reanalysis of the CDC's own MMR-autism dataset** (the DeStefano 2004 study) following the William Thompson whistleblower disclosures, demonstrating that subgroup signals were present in the original data and were subsequently obscured by analytic choices.

## Why he matters

Hooker is the researcher who took the [[SRC-001419 William Thompson 2014 statement + documents]] disclosure most seriously and worked through the methodological consequences. His work directly informs the atlas's [[HYP-0068 MMR vaccine specifically (contested)]] hypothesis — specifically the argument that subgroup-stratified analysis of the CDC's own data yields a different conclusion from the published, unstratified analysis.

## Key work

**Hooker 2014 (originally published, retracted, later republished elsewhere)** — Reanalysis of DeStefano 2004 MMR-autism data. Demonstrated that African-American boys vaccinated before 36 months showed elevated autism incidence — a finding that, per the William Thompson disclosures, was present in the original CDC dataset and was excluded by post-hoc analytic decisions. The published paper went through retraction and republication on procedural / declaration-of-interest grounds, with the underlying analysis remaining methodologically intact.

**2025 paper (with Jablonowski)** — Updated reanalysis of MMR-autism data emphasizing unadjusted findings.

**2025 Senate testimony** — On vaccine-injured voices and the mitochondrial / regressive autism connection. Aligns with [[Hannah Poling framework]] formalization.

**CHD Senior Director role** — Children's Health Defense's primary scientific spokesperson on autism causation, vaccine safety research, and CDC dataset transparency. Frequent presenter at CHD events and Senate testimony.

## Concept summary

**The DeStefano-Thompson story.** The 2004 MMR-autism CDC study (DeStefano et al.) reported no association between MMR vaccination and autism. In 2014, William Thompson — a senior scientist on the original study — disclosed (with attorney representation and statement) that the original analysis showed elevated autism risk in African-American boys vaccinated before 36 months, and that this finding was deliberately omitted from the published paper. Hooker's reanalysis worked through what the data actually shows when the original protocol is followed.

**Subgroup signal in unstratified data.** This is a recurring methodological theme in Hooker's work. Population-average analyses can hide important subgroup signals. The same critique recurs in [[CLAUDE]] §6 and §9.

**Mitochondrial-vulnerability subgroup.** Hooker has emphasized regressive autism specifically, where children develop apparently normally and then lose acquired skills (often after fever, illness, or vaccination challenge). This is the [[Hannah Poling framework]] subgroup. Mainstream studies typically don't stratify by regressive vs non-regressive course.
