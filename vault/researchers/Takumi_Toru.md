# Prof. Toru Takumi

**Affiliation:** Professor, Kobe University Graduate School of Medicine; Senior Visiting Scientist, RIKEN Center for Biosystems Dynamics Research

## Why this researcher matters

Takumi developed the most influential **chromosome-engineered mouse model of autism** — the 15q11-13 duplication mouse — published in *Cell* in 2009. This model is the foundational preclinical tool for studying the most common cytogenetic abnormality in autism (15q11-13 maternal duplication). His more recent identification of **Necdin (NDN)** as a causal gene in this region (Nature Communications 2021) extends the framework to molecular-mechanism precision.

## Key contributions

### 15q11-13 duplication mouse model (2009)

In a landmark *Cell* paper, Takumi's group used chromosome engineering to generate a 6.3 Mb duplication of the conserved linkage group on mouse chromosome 7 — corresponding to human 15q11-13. Maternally-derived duplication mice show:

- **Poor social interaction**
- **Behavioral inflexibility**
- **Abnormal ultrasonic vocalizations**
- **Anxiety-correlated behaviors**

This is the **autosomal-duplication mouse model** that the autism genetics field uses to test 15q11-13 mechanism. 15q11-13 maternal duplication is among the most common cytogenetic findings in autism (~1-3% of cases), making this model translationally relevant.

### Necdin (NDN) as causal gene (2021)

Takumi's 2021 Nature Communications paper identified **Necdin** as a key driver of the autism phenotype within the 15q11-13 duplication. Necdin is a paternally-imprinted gene; maternal duplication alters Necdin dosage, perturbing neural circuit development.

### Neuroanatomical phenotype work

The 15q11-13 duplication mouse shows reproducible **brain structural abnormalities** that parallel human imaging findings — increased cortical volume in some regions, decreased connectivity in others. This work connects [[BIO-0137 MRI cortical thickness]] and [[BIO-0138 Total brain volume / amygdala / cerebellar vermis volumetry]] to specific genetic etiologies.

## Notes

Takumi's lab is at the intersection of mouse genetics + behavioral neuroscience + neuroimaging — making it an unusually translational program. Japanese autism genetics is a strong subfield largely because of work from his group and collaborators at RIKEN.
