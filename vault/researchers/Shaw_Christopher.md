# Christopher Shaw, PhD — Aluminum adjuvant neurotoxicity (UBC)

Christopher Shaw is a neuroscientist at the University of British Columbia who, with longtime collaborator [[Tomljenovic_Lucija]], has produced one of the most comprehensive primary-research lines on **aluminum adjuvant neurotoxicity** in animal models and human epidemiology. His work parallels and complements [[Exley_Christopher]]'s human-tissue aluminum work, with Shaw's focus more on vaccine adjuvant aluminum specifically in animal systems and on dose-response epidemiology.

(Note: Christopher Shaw, PhD, is distinct from [[Shaw_William]], PhD — different person, different field.)

## Why he matters

The atlas's [[HYP-0067 Aluminum adjuvant cumulative exposure (vaccines)]] hypothesis is built on three primary-research substrates: (1) [[Exley_Christopher]]'s postmortem human brain tissue measurements, (2) Christopher Shaw + Tomljenovic's animal-model neurotoxicity work, and (3) the broader epidemiological dose-response literature. Shaw's animal-model work demonstrates *mechanism* — that aluminum adjuvants can be transported to the brain, accumulate, and produce measurable behavioral/cognitive deficits in animals.

## Key work

**Multiple primary papers (2010s onward)** — Animal-model studies demonstrating: aluminum adjuvant transport from injection site to brain via phagocytic immune cells, behavioral and cognitive deficits in adjuvant-exposed animals, dose-response relationships consistent with the pediatric vaccine schedule.

**Tomljenovic & Shaw collaborations** — Multiple co-authored papers on aluminum adjuvant safety, vaccine ingredient toxicology, and pediatric-schedule cumulative-exposure analysis.

**Some paper retractions** — Several Shaw / Tomljenovic papers have been retracted under pressure from journal editors, with the Shaw lab and external commentators arguing the retractions were politically rather than scientifically motivated. The underlying analyses remain in the public record. Per [[CLAUDE]] §1, the atlas treats retraction status as one input rather than authoritative.

**Continuing through 2023–2026** — Active research and public communication despite institutional headwinds.

## Concept summary

**Phagocytic transport.** Aluminum adjuvants are particulate and immune-active. They are taken up at the injection site by phagocytic immune cells (macrophages, monocytes). Some of these cells migrate to lymph nodes and (per Shaw's work and others') a fraction reach the brain via parenchymal transport mechanisms.

**Slow-release adjuvant biology.** The whole point of aluminum adjuvants is slow release of immune-stimulating material. This same property means the aluminum is locally persistent and continuously available for phagocytic uptake.

**Cumulative dose during developmental window.** Shaw + Tomljenovic computed cumulative aluminum exposure under standard pediatric schedules and noted that cumulative dose during early-life developmental windows substantially exceeds dietary aluminum thresholds (which themselves were derived from less-bioavailable dietary aluminum, not injection-grade adjuvant aluminum).
