# Prof. Ilan Dinstein

**Affiliation:** Professor + Director, National Autism Research Center of Israel, Ben-Gurion University of the Negev (BGU); collaborates with Soroka University Medical Center, Be'er Sheva

## Why this researcher matters

Dinstein leads Israel's **National Autism Research Center** — a unique multi-site infrastructure for longitudinal autism research that combines clinical, behavioral, neural, biomarker, and genetic phenotyping in a single national cohort. Israel's small population + universal medical record system make this a methodologically powerful platform comparable to the Swedish national registries. Dinstein's group has been particularly productive on **early-detection biomarkers** and **measuring autism heterogeneity** in clinically meaningful ways.

## Key contributions

### National Autism Research Center

Dinstein's center (BGU + Soroka) operates a longitudinal cohort with:
- Standardized clinical phenotyping at multiple ages
- Eye-tracking + fMRI biomarker collection
- Genetic + epigenetic samples
- Family-history + environmental exposure documentation
- Linkage to Israel's national health database

This is one of the few national-cohort autism research programs that integrates molecular + neural + behavioral data at scale.

### Heterogeneity as primary phenotype dimension

Dinstein has been a leading voice arguing that **autism heterogeneity is the primary signal, not the noise** — i.e., the existence of distinct subtypes with different mechanism + intervention response is the key feature of autism, not a methodological inconvenience. This is a foundational principle of the [[CLAUDE]] §9 atlas framework: mixed-evidence outcomes are almost always effect heterogeneity, not absence of effect.

### Israel autism prevalence trends

Dinstein's group documented that **autism prevalence in Israel nearly doubled between 2017 and 2021** — interpreted (as in most countries) as primarily reflecting improved diagnostic recognition + ascertainment, with a possible smaller true-incidence component.

### Eye-tracking + neural biomarker development

His group has been productive in developing:
- **Pupillometry** measures of autism reactivity ([[BIO-0149]])
- **Eye-tracking attention patterns** ([[BIO-0148]])
- **fMRI heterogeneity quantification** — measuring within-individual neural variability as an autism signature

## Notes

The Azrieli Foundation provided $13M to expand the BGU + Soroka National Autism Research Center in 2024. The Israeli autism research community is small but extremely productive — Dinstein's center, [[Aran_Adi]]'s cannabis trials at Shaare Zedek, and the Weizmann/Tel Aviv neuroscience labs together represent an outsized share of high-impact international autism research.
