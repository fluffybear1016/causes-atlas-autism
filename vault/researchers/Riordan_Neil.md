# Neil Riordan, PhD — Stem Cell Institute Panama UC-MSC therapy

Neil Riordan founded the **Stem Cell Institute Panama** in 2007 and has built one of the world's most-referenced offshore-clinic stem cell programs for pediatric and adult conditions, including autism. His protocol uses **allogeneic umbilical cord mesenchymal stem cells (UC-MSCs)** — donor cells from healthy umbilical cord tissue — administered intravenously in multiple-infusion courses.

For autism specifically, his 2019 published series ([[SRC-001454 31187597]] in *Stem Cells Translational Medicine*) is the most-cited peer-reviewed evidence basis for the offshore Panama-based UC-MSC autism protocols.

## Why he matters

Stem cell therapy for autism in the US is currently limited primarily to:
- Duke trials ([[Kurtzberg_Joanne]] + [[Dawson_Geraldine]]) — the gold standard but enrollment-restricted
- Various commercial clinics with widely variable quality

Riordan's Stem Cell Institute Panama is the most-published offshore option that takes families on a clinical (not trial) basis. It's not as rigorous as Duke trials methodologically, but it has substantially more clinical experience, an established safety record, and protocols documented in peer-reviewed venues.

For families who don't qualify for Duke trials and want UC-MSC therapy under physician supervision with published outcomes, this is the most-evidence-supported commercial option.

## How the Panama protocol differs from Duke

| Dimension | Duke Trials | Stem Cell Institute Panama |
|---|---|---|
| Cell type | Autologous cord blood (Phase 1) + allogeneic cord blood + allogeneic cord tissue MSC | Allogeneic UC-MSC (donor) |
| Regulatory framework | FDA IND, formal RCT/Phase 2 trials | Panamanian medical regulation; not FDA-overseen for US patients |
| Cost | Typically free to participants (research-funded) | $15,000-$30,000 per protocol |
| Selection | Strict trial criteria | Open clinical practice |
| Outcome measurement | Multi-modality (Vineland, behavioral, EEG, eye-tracking, biomarkers) | Clinical observation + parental report; some biomarker tracking |
| Evidence base | Phase 1 + Phase 2 RCT peer-reviewed | Case series peer-reviewed |
| Sample sizes per study | 25 (Phase 1), 180 (Phase 2) | Smaller per publication; larger cumulative clinical experience |

The Riordan Panama protocol is methodologically below Duke trials but **practically more accessible** for families who have financial resources and don't qualify for Duke enrollment.

## Practical takeaway for parents

Stem Cell Institute Panama is a **legitimate-tier 2 commercial option** for cell therapy in autism. It is NOT a research trial — it's clinical practice. Cost is significant. Travel is required (Panama City).

Best fit:
- Family has financial resources ($15-30K + travel)
- Child has documented inflammatory phenotype, regressive course, or mitochondrial vulnerability
- Has not qualified for or is not pursuing Duke trial enrollment
- Family is comfortable with offshore clinical care

Less ideal fit:
- Mild presentation without clear biomarker rationale
- Pure genetic-syndromic case
- First-line interventions not yet exhausted

If Duke trials are an option, that's the gold standard. Riordan's clinic is the most-published commercial second tier.
