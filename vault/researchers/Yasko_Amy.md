# Amy Yasko, NP, PhD — SNP-targeted methylation protocols

Amy Yasko is one of the most influential clinical-protocol architects in functional-medicine autism. Her core contribution is a **systematic, SNP-genotype-driven methylation/transsulfuration protocol** that translates the [[James_Jill]] and [[Walsh_William]] biochemistry frameworks into individualized intervention schedules based on a patient's specific methylation-cycle gene variants (MTHFR, MTRR, MTR, COMT, CBS, BHMT, AHCY, MAT, SUOX, NOS, and others).

## Why she matters

The methylation cycle is gene-controlled: a child's specific SNP profile across 30+ key one-carbon-cycle genes substantially affects which interventions help, which interventions paradoxically worsen things, and what dosing makes sense. Yasko's framework operationalizes this into clinical practice. Whether or not every detail of her protocol scheme is fully validated by mainstream peer-reviewed evidence, her core insight — that genotype matters for nutrient response — is foundational to modern functional-medicine methylation work and is being increasingly validated as the [[Frye_Richard]] and [[James_Jill]] biochemistry layers mature.

## Key work

***Autism: Pathways to Recovery*** (2009) — Comprehensive synthesis of the SNP-targeted methylation framework, with specific protocols. Densely referenced. The book that established Yasko as a primary clinical-protocol voice in functional-medicine autism.

***Genetic Bypass*** — Detailed treatment of methylation-cycle genetics and protocol design.

**Ongoing Yasko Protocol clinical practice and educational infrastructure** — Holistic Health, Inc. supports families and clinicians implementing the protocol. Continuing through 2023–2026.

## Concept summary

**SNP-genotype-driven intervention.** A child with the MTHFR C677T variant cannot efficiently convert folic acid to its active 5-MTHF form, so should receive methylated folate rather than synthetic folic acid. A child with the COMT V158M variant has slower dopamine breakdown, so methyl donor support has to be calibrated carefully. A child with CBS upregulation may have transsulfuration overflow problems that require specific molybdenum/B6/B12 support. The Yasko Protocol systematizes these gene-by-gene response considerations.

**Sequential intervention strategy.** Rather than throwing all methylation-supportive nutrients in at once, the Yasko approach builds sequentially: clear toxicants first, support transsulfuration, support B12 / B6 cofactors, gradually introduce methyl donors with monitoring. This sequencing reduces the "feeling worse before feeling better" reactions that uncalibrated methyl-donor protocols can trigger.

**RNA-based supplementation.** Yasko also developed RNA-based supplementation protocols (a more controversial element) intended to support specific cellular pathways. This is the part of her protocol most distant from mainstream evidence and is treated as such in this atlas.

## Practical takeaway for parents

The Yasko Protocol is widely-used in functional-medicine autism practice but is detailed enough that working with a Yasko-trained or methylation-experienced clinician is generally necessary. **Holistic Health, Inc.** offers educational resources, a SNP analysis service (Nutrigenomic Test Panel), and clinical support.

For parents who want a parallel mainstream-compatible methylation workup, [[James_Jill]]'s biochemistry panel + 23andMe / SelfDecode SNP analysis interpreted by a knowledgeable clinician covers similar ground.
