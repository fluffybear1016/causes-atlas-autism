# Daniel Rossignol, MD — Mitochondrial dysfunction and HBOT in subgroup ASD

Daniel Rossignol is the most prolific autism-focused mitochondrial-dysfunction researcher in clinical practice today, and a frequent collaborator with [[Frye_Richard]]. His core contribution is the systematic documentation that **mitochondrial dysfunction is present in a major subgroup of autistic children — likely 30–50% — and that this subgroup responds to mitochondrial cofactor support and (in select cases) hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT)**.

## Why he matters

Mainstream pediatrics rarely tests autistic children for mitochondrial dysfunction. The Rossignol/Frye line of work establishes that:

1. Mitochondrial biomarkers (lactate, pyruvate, lactate-to-pyruvate ratio, acylcarnitine, CoQ10, creatine kinase) are abnormal in a substantial subset of autistic children even when standard pediatric workup is unremarkable.
2. This subgroup is the same one that overlaps with the [[Hannah Poling framework]] — children with mitochondrial vulnerability who, when challenged with vaccines/infection/fever/metabolic stressors, can have regressive deterioration into the autism phenotype.
3. Mitochondrial cofactor support (CoQ10, L-carnitine, thiamine, riboflavin, niacinamide, biotin, vitamin C, vitamin E, alpha-lipoic acid) shows benefit in the mito-vulnerable subset.
4. HBOT shows variable results in unstratified autism cohorts (consistent with effect heterogeneity per [[CLAUDE]] §9) but more consistent benefit when the cohort is enriched for mitochondrial / oxidative-stress markers.

## Key work

**Rossignol & Frye 2012 (PMID 21263444)** — *"Mitochondrial dysfunction in autism spectrum disorders: a systematic review and meta-analysis."* Molecular Psychiatry. The paper that put the mito-ASD link on the map at scale.

**HBOT trials and systematic reviews** — Multiple pieces. Mixed results in unstratified cohorts. Stronger signal in oxidative-stress / mito-vulnerable subgroups.

**2024 mitochondrial biomarker meta-analysis (with Frye)** — Updated synthesis. Reinforces oxidative stress and inflammation as actionable targets in subgroup-positive ASD.

**Ongoing collaborative work** with Frye on FRAA / leucovorin protocols, where folate-pathway and mitochondrial-pathway dysfunction are biochemically coupled.

## Practical takeaway for parents

If the child has any of: regression after illness/vaccination/fever, low energy / hypotonia, GI dysmotility, recurrent fatigue, family history of mitochondrial disease — get a mitochondrial workup. Lactate, pyruvate, L:P ratio, full acylcarnitine profile, creatine kinase, ammonia, organic acids, and where indicated muscle biopsy or genetic mito-panel. If positive, mitochondrial cofactor support (the "mito cocktail") is well-tolerated and has favorable risk/benefit.

HBOT is more contested — significant cost, conflicting trial results — but is reasonable to consider in oxidative-stress-positive cases under specialist supervision.
