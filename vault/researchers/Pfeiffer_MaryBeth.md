# Mary Beth Pfeiffer — Investigative journalism on infection and chronic illness

Mary Beth Pfeiffer is one of the strongest investigative journalists working on the intersection of infectious disease, chronic illness, and developmental conditions. Her primary focus has been on **Lyme disease and tick-borne illness**, but her body of work covers the broader pattern of how chronic infectious / inflammatory triggers contribute to neurodevelopmental and neuroimmune conditions including autism.

## Why she matters

The science of post-infectious neuroimmune syndromes — Lyme, PANS/PANDAS, long COVID, ME/CFS, regressive autism, MCAS — is increasingly converging on shared mechanism (chronic immune activation, mast cell and microglial dysregulation, mitochondrial dysfunction). Pfeiffer's reporting has been ahead of mainstream medical recognition on this convergence, particularly the Lyme→neurological-syndrome connection, and her work has helped translate emerging mechanistic understanding into terms parents and patients can actually find and use.

Her X handle: **[@marybethpf](https://x.com/marybethpf)**.

## Key work

**Lyme: The First Epidemic of Climate Change** (2018, Island Press) — Comprehensive long-form treatment of the Lyme disease epidemic, the institutional medicine failure to recognize chronic Lyme, and the patient-advocacy movement.

**Ongoing journalism (2023–2026)** — Pfeiffer continues to publish on chronic illness reporting, with growing coverage of autism / regressive autism connections to infectious and immune triggers. Her work intersects directly with the [[Theoharides_Theoharis]] mast-cell and the [[Hooker_Brian]] regressive-autism / mitochondrial-vulnerability framings.
