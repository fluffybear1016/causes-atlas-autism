# Daniel Geschwind, MD, PhD — Autism genetics and brain transcriptomics

Daniel Geschwind runs one of the world's leading mainstream autism genetics programs at UCLA. His lab is responsible for foundational work on the **polygenic and rare-variant genetic architecture of autism**, on **transcriptomic dysregulation in autistic brain tissue**, and on the maintenance of one of the most-used human autism brain banks for downstream research.

## Why he matters (and the limits)

Geschwind's work establishes the **genetic substrate** layer of the atlas's [[Hannah Poling framework]] — the *susceptibility* dimension of P(Φ | susceptibility, exposure). Without large-scale genomics work like Geschwind's, the atlas's [[HYP-0028 Inherited polygenic risk]] hypothesis would have no quantitative foundation. The 1,564 genes currently in the atlas (with 781 SFARI Tier 1+2 genes wired to HYP-0028) come substantially from research programs Geschwind helped lead and standardize.

The mainstream framing limit: Geschwind-class work is genetics-forward and tends to under-weight environmental contribution. The atlas integrates this work as the genetic substrate but places it in the same multi-factor causation framework alongside [[Naviaux_Robert]]'s 3-Hit environmental triggers, [[Frye_Richard]]'s metabolic biomarkers, and the contested-evidence environmental cluster. Mainstream genetics-only framing is not the atlas's framing.

## Key work

**Multiple landmark transcriptomic papers** — *Cell*, *Nature*, *Nature Neuroscience*. Demonstrated systematic dysregulation of synaptic, immune-microglial, and astrocyte gene-expression modules in autistic postmortem brain tissue. These transcriptomic signatures are mechanistically informative and connect the genetic substrate to actual brain biology.

**Brain-bank leadership** — UCLA / Autism BrainNet brain bank coordination is one of the foundational scientific infrastructures for autism postmortem tissue research, including [[Theoharides_Theoharis]]'s mast cell tissue work and [[Exley_Christopher]]'s aluminum tissue work.

**Polygenic risk architecture work** — Quantifying common-variant heritability of autism, GWAS-derived polygenic scores, and how they interact with rare large-effect variants ([[SFARI Tier 1]] high-confidence autism genes).

**Active 2023–2026 research portfolio** — UCLA Institute for Precision Health, where Geschwind serves as senior leadership. Continuing autism genetics, also extending to broader neuropsychiatric genomics.

## Practical takeaway for parents

Genetic testing for autism is increasingly clinically standard and includes:

- **Chromosomal microarray (CMA)** — first-line; detects copy-number variants.
- **Fragile X testing** — FMR1 trinucleotide repeat.
- **Autism gene panels** — clinical-grade panels covering high-confidence (Tier 1) and emerging (Tier 2) autism genes from databases like SFARI Gene that Geschwind-adjacent research populates.
- **Whole-exome / whole-genome sequencing (WES/WGS)** — for unexplained cases.

A positive genetic finding does not change behavioral therapy but does help in some specific ways: predicts comorbidities (e.g., Fragile X kids have specific medical surveillance needs), predicts inheritance risk for siblings, occasionally identifies a gene with a specific responsive intervention (e.g., mTOR-pathway genes that respond to rapamycin — see [[INT-0036 Rapamycin (sirolimus)]]).

This work matters. It just isn't the full picture without the [[Hannah Poling framework]] environmental + metabolic + immune layers.
