# Prof. Tadafumi Kato

**Affiliation:** Professor, Department of Psychiatry and Behavioral Science; Director, Center for Mood Disorders, Juntendo University, Tokyo

## Why this researcher matters

Kato is the leading proponent of the **mitochondrial dysfunction framework for psychiatric disorders** — a framework that intersects directly with [[PHE-0002 Mitochondrial dysfunction phenotype|autism mitochondrial phenotype]]. He discovered mitochondrial dysfunction in postmortem brains of bipolar patients in 2000 and has spent two decades extending the framework to neuropsychiatric disease broadly. His 2023 work demonstrating that **autism-spectrum genes are enriched for deleterious mosaic mutations in bipolar disorder** unifies autism and bipolar genetics across a shared mosaic-mutation spectrum.

## Key contributions

### Mitochondrial framework for psychiatric disorders

Kato's foundational 2000 paper documented dysfunctional mitochondria in postmortem brains of bipolar patients. His subsequent work has shown:

- **Mitochondrial gene expression alterations** in bipolar + schizophrenia postmortem brains
- **Paraventricular thalamic nucleus (PVT) mitochondrial dysfunction** drives mood episodes in mouse models (2016 + 2026 work)
- The framework parallels [[Naviaux_Robert]]'s Cell Danger Response in autism

### Mosaic mutations connecting bipolar + autism

Kato + Atsushi Takata (RIKEN) published landmark work in *Molecular Psychiatry* (2023) showing that **deleterious mosaic mutations in neurodevelopmental disorder genes are enriched in bipolar disorder patients**. This bridges autism + bipolar genetic landscapes through a shared post-zygotic mutation mechanism.

Implications for autism atlas:
- Mosaic mutations are one mechanism by which autism + bipolar can co-occur in families with no obvious germline genetic burden
- Postnatal somatic mutation events in neurodevelopmental genes shape both phenotypes
- Future personalized risk modeling needs to capture mosaic, not just germline, variation

### Mitochondrial-tRNA region enrichment

The same 2023 work identified **enrichment of deleterious mosaic variants in mitochondrial tRNA regions** in bipolar disorder — directly parallel to autism mitochondrial dysfunction findings.

## Notes

Kato's framework is unusually mechanistic for psychiatry. His lab uses iPSC-derived neurons + mouse models + postmortem genomics — a multi-modal approach that mirrors what autism research has converged on.
