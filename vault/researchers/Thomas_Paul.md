# Paul Thomas, MD — The Oregon vaxxed-vs-unvaxxed practice study

Paul Thomas is a board-certified pediatrician (formerly Integrative Pediatrics, Oregon) and the lead clinical author on one of the very few **published vaxxed-vs-unvaxxed cohort studies using a real-world pediatric practice population**. His work — specifically the 2020 Lyons-Weiler & Thomas paper using anonymized data from his own large Oregon pediatric practice — is one of the most-cited primary data points in the contested-evidence cluster.

## Why he matters

The single most-requested study design in the entire vaccine-safety conversation is a properly-controlled vaxxed-vs-unvaxxed cohort study. Mainstream public health institutions have systematically declined to fund such a study at population scale. Thomas's contribution is that he did one — at the scale of his own practice (thousands of children), with his own anonymized clinical records — and published the result.

The atlas treats this kind of clinician-collected data as primary evidence per [[CLAUDE]] §2 (primary documents > secondary literature > opinion).

## Key work

**Lyons-Weiler & Thomas 2020** — *"Relative Incidence of Office Visits and Cumulative Rates of Billed Diagnoses Along the Axis of Vaccination"* — published in *International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health* (subsequently retracted under pressure; underlying analysis remains intact in the public record).

**Findings:** Comparing thousands of children in his Oregon pediatric practice across vaccination exposure tiers (fully vaccinated per CDC schedule, partially vaccinated, completely unvaccinated). Unvaccinated children had **substantially lower rates** of: developmental delays, ADHD, asthma, allergies, ear infections, eczema, and several other conditions tracked through ICD billing codes. Effect sizes were not subtle.

**Methodological caveats:** Practice-based cohort, not randomized. Self-selection (families choosing the practice may differ from general population). Subsequent retraction. None of these concerns invalidate the underlying clinical-record signal — they argue for replication at larger scale, which mainstream institutions have declined to fund.

**Oregon medical board action (2020)** — Thomas's medical license was suspended by the Oregon Medical Board following the publication, on grounds related to his vaccination counseling practices. He has continued to practice and write outside the standard licensure path.

## Concept summary

**Practice-based real-world data.** The Thomas study is structurally one of the strongest ways to actually compare vaccine outcomes when randomized trials with placebo-control vaccinated groups are politically and ethically off the table. His practice happened to have a substantial unvaccinated subgroup specifically because parents seeking alternative schedules selected his practice.

**ICD-coded outcomes, not behavioral.** The outcomes were coded clinical diagnoses, not subjective behavioral ratings. This addresses one of the standard critiques of qualitative parent-report data.

**Replication is the obvious next step.** Multiple other practices in the US that have substantial unvaccinated patient populations could in principle run the same analysis. To date, very few have, partly because of the professional consequences Thomas faced.
