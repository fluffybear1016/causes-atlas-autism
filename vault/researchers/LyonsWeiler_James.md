# James Lyons-Weiler, PhD — Gene-environment computational synthesis

James Lyons-Weiler is a computational biologist who runs the Institute for Pure and Applied Knowledge (IPAK), an independent research nonprofit. His autism contribution is a sustained synthesis argument: **autism etiology is best understood as gene-environment interaction at the population level, and the dominant framing of autism as primarily genetic is computationally and historically incorrect.**

## Why he matters

Lyons-Weiler is one of the few researchers with formal computational-biology training operating in the contested-evidence cluster. His critiques of mainstream autism epidemiology are methodologically grounded rather than ideological. He has consistently pushed for:

- Larger and better-controlled vaxxed-vs-unvaxxed cohort studies.
- True root-cause investigation rather than genetics-only research framing.
- Open data and replication norms.
- Accountability for funding-asymmetry distortions in published epidemiology.

## Key work

**2016 book** — *The Environmental and Genetic Causes of Autism.* Comprehensive synthesis of the gene-environment interaction case for autism. The framework book that anchors his position.

**IPAK research outputs** — Various critiques, reanalyses, and methodological pieces. Frequently published in independent venues rather than mainstream journals (per Lyons-Weiler's own commentary on funding and editorial gatekeeping).

**X feed (2023–2026)** — Active. Defends biomedical autism research, critiques mainstream epidemiology, comments on regulatory and policy developments.
