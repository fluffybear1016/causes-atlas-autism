# Bob Sears, MD — The alternative vaccination schedule

Bob Sears is a board-certified pediatrician (Sears Family Pediatrics, Capistrano Beach CA) and the author of *The Vaccine Book* (2007, multiple updated editions). His core contribution to autism prevention is a **structured alternative pediatric vaccination schedule** that spaces aluminum-adjuvanted vaccines, delays specific vaccines into low-risk windows, and provides parents with a concrete framework for informed-consent pediatric vaccine decisions.

## Why he matters

The atlas treats [[HYP-0066 Hepatitis B vaccine (neonatal birth-dose)]] and [[HYP-0067 Aluminum adjuvant cumulative exposure (vaccines)]] as `status: contested`. Sears was among the first US pediatricians to publish a structured alternative schedule — drawing on [[Exley_Christopher]]'s aluminum work, the [[Hannah Poling framework]] mitochondrial-vulnerability principle, and the broader case that cumulative early-life immunological burden during the critical neurodevelopmental window matters more than the standard schedule acknowledges.

His framework is operational, not just theoretical: a parent walking into a pediatric appointment can use *The Vaccine Book* as a roadmap to informed-consent conversation.

## Key work

***The Vaccine Book*** (2007, with updates) — Comprehensive parent-facing guide to each vaccine in the pediatric schedule, with the alternative-schedule framework. Densely referenced. One of the most consequential consumer-facing pediatric vaccine books ever published.

**Sears Family Pediatrics** — Clinical practice operating under informed-consent / alternative-schedule framework. Has been one of the most influential model practices for pediatricians who want to offer schedule flexibility.

**California professional discipline (2018)** — Sears was placed on probation by the Medical Board of California for issuing vaccine medical exemptions. The probation is part of the broader pattern of state-level mandate enforcement that has affected functional/integrative pediatricians offering non-standard schedules. He has continued practicing throughout.

## Concept summary

**The alternative schedule.** Spaces aluminum-adjuvanted vaccines so that no single appointment delivers more than one. Delays hepatitis B birth-dose for low-risk families (consistent with the international standard in Norway, Netherlands, parts of Scandinavia — see [[HYP-0066]]). Delays MMR until at least 12–15 months. Adjusts for individual family genetic and medical history.

**Informed consent as standard of care.** The Sears framework is, fundamentally, a return to the informed-consent norm that applies in every other area of pediatric medicine. Parents are given the actual risk/benefit analysis for each vaccine including ingredients, frequency of adverse events, and the case for and against the standard timing.

**Personalization by family medical history.** Strong family history of autoimmune disease, mitochondrial disease, regression after prior vaccination, or related red flags warrant schedule modification. This aligns with the [[Hannah Poling framework]] individual-level conditional-risk principle.
