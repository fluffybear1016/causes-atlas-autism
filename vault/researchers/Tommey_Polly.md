# Polly Tommey — Documentary aggregation and parent-voice infrastructure

Polly Tommey is a British documentary filmmaker and broadcaster, mother of an autistic son, and one of the central figures in the vaccine-injured-family documentation movement. She co-produced the *Vaxxed* documentary (2016, with Andrew Wakefield as director) and *Vaxxed II* (2019), and currently runs much of the [CHD.TV](https://chd.tv) programming that aggregates first-person family stories.

## Why she matters

The contested-evidence cluster ([[HYP-0044]] and adjacent) is heavily reliant on case-series and qualitative pattern data — there are thousands of families who report developmental regression with temporal correlation to vaccination. Mainstream medicine has systematically discounted this data class as anecdotal. Tommey's documentary work is the primary infrastructure converting individual family experience into a coherent, searchable corpus that the broader research and policy communities can actually engage with.

She is not a researcher in the laboratory sense. She is a documentation-infrastructure-builder. Different role, complementary contribution.

## Key work

**Vaxxed (2016) and Vaxxed II (2019)** — Documentary films covering the William Thompson disclosures ([[SRC-001419 William Thompson 2014 statement + documents]]) and broader patterns of post-vaccination regression. *Vaxxed II* specifically focused on cross-country bus tour interviews with thousands of vaccine-injured families.

**CHD.TV programming (ongoing)** — Tommey hosts and produces multiple shows on Children's Health Defense's media platform, with frequent autism-specific programming. Includes long-form interviews with families, researchers (many in this directory), and clinicians.

**Vaxxed bus / parent aggregation** — Multi-year project documenting first-person family accounts at scale. The cumulative archive is one of the largest qualitative datasets on temporal vaccine-regression patterns in existence.
