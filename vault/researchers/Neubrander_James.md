# James Neubrander, MD — The methyl-B12 protocol pioneer

James Neubrander is the integrative physician who developed and popularized the **subcutaneous methylcobalamin (methyl-B12) protocol** for autism — now widely known as the "Neubrander Protocol." Over 25+ years of clinical practice, Neubrander has documented thousands of children responding to a specific dosing and frequency regimen of methyl-B12 injections, and has trained hundreds of integrative physicians on the protocol.

## Why he matters

Methyl-B12 deficiency or dysregulation affects a substantial subgroup of autistic children. Standard oral / sublingual B12 supplementation often doesn't reach therapeutic levels in this subgroup — particularly for kids with intracellular methylation cycle defects. Subcutaneous methyl-B12 administration bypasses absorption issues and delivers the active methyl-donor form directly.

Neubrander's contribution is the **specific protocol** — dose, frequency, timing — that produces clinically meaningful response. His framework has become standard in MAPS-trained physician practice and has been integrated into the broader functional medicine framework alongside [[Frye_Richard]]'s leucovorin work and [[James_Jill]]'s biochemistry foundation.

## The Neubrander Protocol

Standard parameters (as widely deployed in MAPS practice):

- **Form:** Methylcobalamin (the active methyl-donor form), preservative-free injectable solution
- **Concentration:** 25 mg/mL (compounded preparation)
- **Dose:** ~64.5 mcg/kg per injection
- **Frequency:** Every 3 days (every-third-day)
- **Route:** Subcutaneous injection (typically into upper outer buttock)
- **Trial duration:** Minimum 4-6 weeks before assessing response; many children respond gradually over months

**Common observations during response:**
- Increased eye contact
- Improved language production / receptive language
- More social engagement
- Better attention
- Some children: temporary increased emotional expression / "frustration" as language emerges

**Typical workup before starting:**
- Plasma B12, methylmalonic acid (MMA)
- Plasma SAM/SAH ratio
- Methylation-cycle SNP profile (especially MTRR, MTR, MTRR)
- FRAA antibody panel (often combined with [[Frye_Richard]] leucovorin protocol)

## Practical takeaway for parents

If your child has documented methylation cycle dysfunction (low SAM/SAH ratio, elevated homocysteine, B12-related metabolic markers), the Neubrander Protocol is one of the most-experienced clinical interventions available. It requires:

- **Knowledgeable prescriber** — MAPS-trained physician familiar with the protocol
- **Compounded methylcobalamin** — most pharmacies don't carry it; compounding pharmacy needed
- **Family willingness to give SC injections** — every 3 days, at home
- **Patience** — response is gradual; often months not weeks

Many children respond dramatically. Some don't. The response profile correlates with methylation biotype + FRAA status + broader biochemical context.

## Note

Neubrander is fundamentally a clinical practitioner rather than a primary-research investigator. His contribution is operational — translating the methylation-cycle biochemistry of [[James_Jill]] and others into a deployable clinical protocol. The protocol is widely used but not extensively documented in large RCT format; the evidence base is decades of clinical experience and the underlying biochemistry of methylcobalamin in methylation cycle support.
