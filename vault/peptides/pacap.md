# PACAP (Pituitary Adenylate Cyclase-Activating Polypeptide)

> Developmental Neuropeptide · evidence tier `endogenous_signaling_research_only_clinical`

**Atlas:** _not yet promoted — vault-only research scaffold; needs PMID-verified evidence for atlas promotion_

## Mechanism of action

38- or 27-amino-acid neuropeptide (PACAP-38 dominant CNS form). Acts on PAC1, VPAC1, VPAC2 receptors. Critical role in fetal brain development — neurogenesis, neuroprotection, glial differentiation. Knockout mice show neurodevelopmental + behavioral phenotypes relevant to autism. **Endogenous neurodevelopmental signaling, not an established intervention.**
