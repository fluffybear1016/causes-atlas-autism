# 👋 Parent Quick Start

You're here because you have a child on the autism spectrum and you're looking for something more than just "behavioral therapy and wait." Good. There's a lot more available than mainstream pediatrics tends to mention.

This page is your 5-minute orientation. After reading this, you'll know enough to navigate the rest of the atlas and start asking your child's clinician the right questions.

## 🎯 The single most important idea

**Autism is not one thing.** It's many overlapping subsets of children, each with different biology, each potentially responsive to different interventions. The job is to figure out *which subset your specific child is in* — not to treat autism generically.

The technical name for this is the **Hannah Poling framework**: causation = susceptibility × trigger → mechanism → phenotype. For *your* child specifically, the question is which susceptibility your child has, what triggered it, what mechanism is currently active, and what phenotype that's producing.

## 🔍 The 6 most common subgroups

Look at your child. Which of these sound most like them? (Many kids fit more than one.)

### 1. 🦠 Microbiome / GI subgroup

**Signs:** GI problems (constipation, diarrhea, reflux), strong food sensitivities, recurring rashes/eczema, history of antibiotic exposure, behavior changes that track with diet.

**What to read:** [[topics/interventions/microbiome/00_MICROBIOME_INTERVENTIONS_OVERVIEW]]

**Top researcher:** [[Hazan_Sabine]] (familial FMT pioneer; ProgenaBiome) and [[Adams_James]] (peer-reviewed MTT trials at ASU)

**Top first step:** Get the Mosaic OAT (Organic Acids Test) urine panel + GI-MAP stool panel. Find a functional-medicine GI specialist.

### 2. 🧪 Folate / methylation subgroup

**Signs:** Language delay or regression, behavioral irritability, family history of MTHFR variants, ADHD-overlap features, response (positive or negative) to dietary folate changes.

**What to read:** [[topics/interventions/methylation_pathway/00_METHYLATION_OVERVIEW]]

**Top researcher:** [[Frye_Richard]] (FRAA/leucovorin), [[James_Jill]] (foundational biochemistry), [[Walsh_William]] (biotyping), [[Yasko_Amy]] (SNP-targeted)

**Top first step:** Get the FRAA antibody test + plasma SAM/SAH ratio + methylation-cycle SNP panel (23andMe + interpretation). Find a methylation-experienced clinician.

### 3. ⚡ Mitochondrial / energy subgroup

**Signs:** Low muscle tone, fatigue, regression after illness/fever/vaccination, family history of mitochondrial issues, GI dysmotility.

**What to read:** [[topics/interventions/mitochondrial/00_MITOCHONDRIAL_OVERVIEW]]

**Top researcher:** [[Rossignol_Daniel]], [[Naviaux_Robert]] (3-Hit framework + suramin)

**Top first step:** Get lactate, pyruvate, L:P ratio, full acylcarnitine profile, organic acids panel. Find a mitochondrial-medicine specialist.

### 4. 🛡️ Immune / inflammatory subgroup

**Signs:** Chronic allergies/eczema/asthma, MCAS features (flushing, abdominal pain, dysautonomia), regression following infection or vaccination, autoimmune family history, sudden-onset OCD/tics (PANS/PANDAS).

**What to read:** [[topics/interventions/immune_modulation/00_IMMUNE_OVERVIEW]]

**Top researcher:** [[Theoharides_Theoharis]] (mast cells), [[Swedo_Sue]] + [[Cunningham_Madeleine]] (PANS/PANDAS)

**Top first step:** Cytokine panel + tryptase + Cunningham Panel if PANS features. Find an MCAS-experienced or PANS-experienced clinician.

### 5. 🧬 Genetic / monogenic subgroup

**Signs:** Multi-organ involvement, distinctive physical features, severe presentation, family history of similar cases, syndromic comorbidities (epilepsy, intellectual disability).

**What to read:** [[Genomics_of_Autism]]

**Top researcher:** [[Geschwind_Daniel]], [[Chung_Wendy]], [[Hollander_Eric]]

**Top first step:** Get CMA + Fragile X + autism gene panel + whole-exome sequencing. Find a clinical geneticist.

### 6. 🌍 Environmental / regressive subgroup

**Signs:** Lost developmental skills (started talking, then stopped; engaged socially, then withdrew), regression timed with specific exposure, severe presentation that wasn't always present.

**What to read:** [[topics/interventions/cell_therapy/00_CELL_THERAPY_OVERVIEW|Cell therapy overview]] + [[topics/interventions/immune_modulation/00_IMMUNE_OVERVIEW|Immune modulation overview]] + [[Stem_Cell_Therapy_for_Autism]] (deep dive)

**Top researcher:** [[Hooker_Brian]], [[Naviaux_Robert]] (3-Hit framework), [[Kurtzberg_Joanne]] + [[Dawson_Geraldine]] (Duke cord blood trials). Read [[Hannah Poling framework]] for the central organizing principle.

**Top first step:** Comprehensive workup across all of the above (cytokines + mitochondrial markers + immune panel + microbiome). Strong candidate for stem cell therapy under FDA IND (Duke trials) or commercial UC-MSC (Stem Cell Institute Panama under [[Riordan_Neil]]).

## 🚦 What to do FIRST (regardless of subgroup)

These tests should be in essentially every autistic child's workup and are widely insurance-covered. Each links to the biomarker entity-page in the atlas with full mechanism + intervention wiring:

1. **Genetic basics** — Chromosomal microarray + Fragile X + Autism gene panel + [[BIO-0161 MTHFR C677T genotype]]
2. **Methylation cycle** — [[BIO-0003 SAM_SAH ratio]] + [[BIO-0005 Homocysteine]] + [[BIO-0009 Plasma B12 (cobalamin)]] + [[BIO-0011 Plasma folate]] + [[BIO-0012 RBC folate]] + [[BIO-0015 FRAA (folate receptor alpha autoantibody) — blocking]]
3. **Inflammation** — [[BIO-0046 High-sensitivity CRP (hs-CRP)]] + [[BIO-0048 IL-6 (interleukin-6)]] + [[BIO-0049 TNF-α (tumor necrosis factor alpha)]] + [[BIO-0062 Tryptase (baseline)]]
4. **Mitochondrial** — [[BIO-0018 Lactate]] + [[BIO-0019 Pyruvate]] + [[BIO-0020 Lactate-to-pyruvate ratio (L_P)]] + [[BIO-0021 Acylcarnitine profile]]
5. **Vitamin status** — [[BIO-0165 Vitamin D 25-OH (child _ direct)]] + [[BIO-0103 Ferritin]]
6. **Microbiome** — [[BIO-0178 Comprehensive Stool Analysis (GI-MAP _ CDSA composite)]]
7. **Functional metabolomics** — [[BIO-0035 Arabinose]] + [[BIO-0036 HPHPA (3-(3-hydroxyphenyl)-3-hydroxypropionic acid)]] + [[BIO-0037 4-cresol (p-cresol)]] (via Mosaic OAT)
8. **Heavy metal screen** — [[BIO-0074 Hair mercury]] + [[BIO-0166 Urinary porphyrins (heavy metal body burden surrogate)]]

For complete biomarker catalog organized by clinical question see **[[topics/biomarkers/00_BIOMARKERS_INDEX]]**.

Most tests run $50-400 each. Comprehensive workup typically $1,500-2,500. Insurance covers some.

## 👨‍⚕️ Finding the right clinician

Mainstream pediatricians are usually not trained in this stuff. You want one of:

- **MAPS-trained physician** (Medical Academy of Pediatric Special Needs)
- **Functional medicine pediatrician** (Cleveland Clinic Center for Functional Medicine, Bock Integrative Medicine, similar)
- **Walsh-trained physician** (Mensah Medical, similar)
- **Disease-specific specialist** (PANS specialist, mast cell specialist, mitochondrial specialist)

Not all are equal. Ask: "Will you order [the specific tests] and interpret them?" If they won't, find someone who will.
