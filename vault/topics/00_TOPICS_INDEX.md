# 🧭 Autism Atlas — Topics Master Index

**👋 If you're a parent and overwhelmed, read [[01_PARENT_QUICK_START]] first.** This page is the directory.

This atlas is a living, evidence-driven framework for understanding autism — its causes, mechanisms, biomarkers, and treatments. It's organized so that:

- A **parent** can land here, find their child's most likely subgroup, and identify the highest-leverage next step.
- A **clinician** can navigate to specific intervention categories and find evidence-based protocols.
- A **researcher** can drill into the underlying multi-omics, mechanism, and primary literature.

The atlas's six entity types (auto-generated) live at the top level of the vault: `hypotheses/`, `mechanisms/`, `interventions/`, `phenotypes/`, `genes/`, `sources/`. This `topics/` folder contains **hand-written expert deep-dives** that synthesize across those entities.

## 📚 How to use this atlas

**For a parent making decisions for one specific child:**

1. Read [[01_PARENT_QUICK_START]] for the decision tree.
2. Use the [[Hannah Poling framework]] to frame your child as: susceptibility (genetic/inherited) × trigger (environmental) → mechanism → phenotype.
3. Drill into the interventions class most aligned with your child's clinical picture (inflammatory → microbiome + immune; regression → cell therapy + functional medicine; sensory/seizure → GABA/methylation; etc.).
4. Pick a clinician from the [[00_RESEARCHERS_INDEX]] who specializes in that mechanism.

**For a clinician exploring options:**

1. Use [[topics/interventions/00_INTERVENTIONS_INDEX]] as the catalog.
2. Cross-reference each candidate with the underlying omics evidence in [[topics/omics/00_OMICS_INDEX]].
3. Use the [[CLAUDE]] §9 effect-heterogeneity principle for stratification logic.

**For a researcher exploring evidence:**

1. Walk the entity graph: each HYP-XXXX, MEC-XXXX, INT-XXXX, PHE-XXXX has its own auto-generated page with full evidence_links to primary sources.
2. Multi-omics integration evidence is concentrated in [[topics/omics/]].
3. Source quality framework lives in [[CLAUDE]] §1-§4.

## 🌱 Why we built this

Mainstream autism medicine in 2026 still hands parents a behavioral diagnosis and a referral to ABA. This atlas exists because that's not enough — there is real, replicable, biomarker-driven, mechanism-targeted, peer-reviewed-supported work that produces meaningful improvement in stratified subgroups, and parents need a navigable map of it.

The atlas is also designed to be **structurally reusable for any medical condition**. The 6-entity-type schema (Hypothesis × Mechanism × Phenotype × Intervention × Gene × Source) plus the topics/ folder organization scales to any complex chronic disease. This is autism today; it could be Alzheimer's, depression, autoimmunity, or anything else tomorrow.
