# ⚡ Mitochondrial Interventions for Autism — Overview

Mitochondrial dysfunction is documented in **30-50% of autistic children** per [[Rossignol_Daniel]] + [[Frye_Richard]] meta-analyses. Mitochondria are the cellular powerplants — they produce ATP, regulate calcium signaling, control apoptosis, and (per [[Naviaux_Robert]]'s framework) gate the cell danger response. When mitochondria are vulnerable or stressed, the developing brain runs short of energy at exactly the wrong moments.

This is a high-priority class for any child with regressive course, post-illness deterioration, low muscle tone, or family history of mitochondrial issues.

## When to consider this class

Strong indication:
- Regression after illness, fever, vaccination, surgery, or other physiological stressor (the [[Hannah Poling framework]] subgroup)
- Low muscle tone (hypotonia)
- Fatigue / low endurance
- Multiple regressions or plateaus
- Family history of mitochondrial disease, including in mother (mtDNA inherited maternally)
- Elevated lactate or pyruvate
- Abnormal acylcarnitine profile
- GI dysmotility

Weaker indication:
- High-functioning, communicative autism without metabolic markers
- Pure microbiome-driven case (microbiome class first)

## The mitochondrial cocktail

The well-established "mito cocktail" — clinically used for both formal mitochondrial disease and autism with mitochondrial features. Multiple ingredients work synergistically; partial protocols are less effective.

**Core components:**

- **CoQ10 (ubiquinol form preferred)** — direct electron transport chain support. Dose: 100-300 mg/day (children); 200-600 mg/day (older).
- **L-Carnitine** — fatty acid transport into mitochondria. Acetyl-L-carnitine for BBB-crossing. Dose: 250-1000 mg twice daily.
- **B-vitamin complex** — multiple roles in Krebs cycle:
  - Thiamine (B1) — pyruvate dehydrogenase cofactor; high-dose (300-1000 mg) for thiamine-responsive dysfunction
  - Riboflavin (B2 / R5P) — complex I and II cofactor; high-dose for ETC deficiencies
  - Niacinamide (B3) — NAD+ precursor
  - Pantothenic acid (B5) — CoA synthesis
  - Pyridoxal-5-phosphate (P5P / B6) — many enzymatic roles
  - Methylcobalamin (B12) — see [[topics/interventions/methylation_pathway/00_METHYLATION_OVERVIEW]]
- **Vitamin C** — antioxidant; mitochondrial respiratory chain support. 500-2000 mg/day.
- **Vitamin E (mixed tocopherols)** — membrane-protective antioxidant. 200-400 IU/day.
- **Alpha-lipoic acid** — universal antioxidant; recycles glutathione + vitamin C + vitamin E. 100-600 mg/day.
- **Creatine** — cellular energy buffering. 1-5 g/day.
- **D-Ribose** — direct ATP precursor. 5 g 1-3x daily, especially for fatigue.
- **Magnesium** — many mitochondrial enzymes require Mg. Mg-glycinate, Mg-malate, Mg-threonate (BBB-crossing).

This is the standard [[Rossignol_Daniel]] / mainstream mitochondrial-medicine starter cocktail.

## Advanced mitochondrial support

For more aggressive intervention or refractory cases:

- **PQQ (pyrroloquinoline quinone)** — promotes mitochondrial biogenesis (more mitochondria, not just better function). 10-20 mg/day.
- **MitoQ** — mitochondria-targeted CoQ10 analog. More specific delivery.
- **NAD+ precursors** — NMN (nicotinamide mononucleotide), NR (nicotinamide riboside). 250-500 mg/day. Boost cellular NAD+ levels.
- **Hyperbaric oxygen therapy (HBOT)** — increased oxygen tension drives mitochondrial recovery. Mixed RCT results overall; better in oxidative-stress-positive subgroups. Cost-prohibitive but accessible at dedicated clinics.
- **Methylene blue (low-dose)** — alternative electron carrier; emerging research.
- **Trehalose** — autophagy / mitophagy promoter.
- **Spermidine** — autophagy promoter, mitochondrial recycling.

## Anti-CDR / antipurinergic interventions (Naviaux framework)

Per [[Naviaux_Robert]]'s 3-Hit framework, persistent activation of the cell danger response (CDR) is what drives the "stuck" metabolic state. Antipurinergic interventions theoretically interrupt this:

- **Suramin (low-dose)** — antipurinergic agent; PMID 28695149 Naviaux 2017 phase I/II RCT showed core symptom improvements. Toxicity profile limits broad clinical use; not commercially available for autism in US.
- **Pyrimidine + purine modulators** — emerging research; not clinically available yet.

## Avoidance — what NOT to give

Mitochondrial-vulnerable children are sensitive to certain medications and exposures:

**Avoid where possible:**
- **Valproate / valproic acid** — strongly mito-toxic; can trigger regression
- **Acetaminophen (Tylenol)** — depletes glutathione, mito-stressing in vulnerable kids; emerging evidence
- **Statins** — mitochondrial CoQ10 depletion
- **Aminoglycoside antibiotics** — mito-toxic
- **Long-term metformin** in already mito-compromised (despite metformin's general utility — context-dependent)
- **Anesthesia** when avoidable; if necessary, plan with mito-aware anesthesiologist
- **Severe physiological stressors** — fasting, severe illness, dehydration

**Reduce environmental load:**
- Glyphosate (per [[Seneff_Stephanie]])
- Heavy metals (mercury, lead, aluminum)
- Air pollution
- Mold exposure
- Excessive EMF

## Life-stage-specific protocols

Per CLAUDE.md Session 3.5H roadmap (planned expansion):

**Preconception:**
- Both parents on mitochondrial cocktail 6 months before conception
- Maternal mitochondrial workup if family history
- Avoidance of mitotoxic exposures and medications

**Pregnancy:**
- Continued maternal mito cocktail (calibrated to pregnancy-safe doses)
- DHA / omega-3 sufficiency (foundational neurodevelopmental component)
- Avoidance of mitotoxic medications (especially valproate)

**Birth and early postnatal:**
- Vaginal birth where possible (microbiome seeding has metabolic implications)
- Breastfeeding (mitochondrially superior to formula in known ways)
- Avoidance of unnecessary medical interventions

**Infancy and early childhood:**
- Begin mitochondrial workup at first sign of metabolic vulnerability (hypotonia, poor feeding, regression)
- Mito cocktail (pediatric-dosed) if indicated
- Avoidance of mitotoxic exposures during the [[HYP-0073 Developmental timing state-transition disorder]] critical window

## Workup

Before starting:

**Foundational:**
- **Lactate** (resting and post-exercise) — elevated suggests anaerobic shift
- **Pyruvate** — paired with lactate
- **Lactate-to-pyruvate ratio** — >25 strongly suggests mito dysfunction
- **Acylcarnitine profile (ACP)** — fatty acid oxidation defects
- **Carnitine (free + total)** — substrate sufficiency
- **Ammonia** — urea cycle / mitochondrial overflow marker
- **Creatine kinase (CK)** — muscle metabolism
- **Mosaic OAT** — covers many mitochondrial markers (citric acid cycle metabolites)
- **CoQ10 level** (if affordable; $$)

**For deeper workup:**
- **Mitochondrial DNA copy number**
- **Genetic mitochondrial panel** — POLG, MT-* mutations, complex deficiency genes
- **Muscle biopsy** with respiratory chain enzyme analysis (last resort)

**Maternal workup if family history:**
- Maternal lactate/pyruvate
- Maternal mtDNA panel
- Maternal symptom history (chronic fatigue, exercise intolerance, family history of "mystery illnesses")

## Bottom line

Mitochondrial intervention is a high-leverage, well-tolerated, broadly-supported class for the substantial mito-vulnerable autism subgroup. The mito cocktail has decades of clinical experience and favorable risk/benefit. Workup is accessible. For the right child, results can be dramatic.

Combined with methylation pathway work and microbiome restoration, this is one of the three foundational biomedical pillars for stratified autism intervention.
