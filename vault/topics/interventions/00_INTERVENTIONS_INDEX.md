# 💊 Interventions — Master Index

This is the catalog of *what to actually do* about autism, organized by mechanism class. Each subfolder has:

- **00_OVERVIEW.md** — class-level synthesis (what this category is, who responds, what the evidence is)
- **Per-intervention deep-dives** — one page per drug, peptide, protocol, or supplement with mechanism, evidence, dosing, monitoring, contraindications

**For parents:** Match your child's clinical picture (per [[01_PARENT_QUICK_START]]) to one or two of the categories below, then read the overview, then drill into specific interventions.

**For clinicians:** Use this as a structured catalog for protocol design. Each intervention deep-dive includes evidence strength, responder profile, monitoring requirements.

## 🗂️ The 8 intervention classes

### 🦠 Microbiome interventions

For children with GI symptoms, food sensitivities, behavior tied to diet, history of antibiotic exposure.

→ **[[topics/interventions/microbiome/00_MICROBIOME_INTERVENTIONS_OVERVIEW|Microbiome interventions overview]]**

Includes: FMT (fecal microbiota transplantation), MTT (Microbiota Transfer Therapy — Adams), targeted probiotics, prebiotics, antifungal protocols, antimicrobial protocols, postbiotics.

**Key deep-dive: [[Hazan_Familial_FMT_Protocol]]** — sibling-donor FMT under FDA IND.

### 🧪 Methylation pathway interventions

For children with language delay, methylation cycle abnormalities, MTHFR variants, low SAM/SAH ratio.

→ **[[topics/interventions/methylation_pathway/00_METHYLATION_OVERVIEW|Methylation pathway interventions overview]]**

Includes: Leucovorin (folinic acid), methyl-B12 (subcutaneous, oral, sublingual), methylated folate (5-MTHF), SAM-e, betaine (TMG), Walsh biotype-stratified protocols, Yasko SNP-targeted protocols.

### ⚡ Mitochondrial interventions

For children with low muscle tone, regression after illness/fever, fatigue, mito family history.

→ **[[topics/interventions/mitochondrial/00_MITOCHONDRIAL_OVERVIEW|Mitochondrial interventions overview]]**

Includes: Mito cofactor cocktail (CoQ10, L-carnitine, B-vitamins, vitamin C/E, alpha-lipoic acid, creatine), PQQ, NAD precursors (NMN, NR), MitoQ, life-stage-specific protocols (preconception, pregnancy, postnatal, childhood).

### 🛡️ Immune modulation interventions

For children with chronic allergies, MCAS features, sudden-onset OCD/tics (PANS/PANDAS), regressive course.

→ **[[topics/interventions/immune_modulation/00_IMMUNE_OVERVIEW|Immune modulation interventions overview]]**

Includes: LDN (low-dose naltrexone), IVIG, plasmapheresis, antihistamines (H1+H2), cromolyn sodium, ketotifen, luteolin, quercetin, antibiotic prophylaxis (PANDAS).

### 🧬 Cell therapy

For children with regressive course, inflammatory phenotype, treatment-plateau on first-line interventions.

→ **[[topics/interventions/cell_therapy/00_CELL_THERAPY_OVERVIEW|Cell therapy overview]]**

Includes: Autologous cord blood (Duke trials), allogeneic UC-MSC (Riordan/Panama), bone marrow MSC, cord tissue MSC, MSC-derived exosomes (emerging).

**Key deep-dive: [[Stem_Cell_Therapy_for_Autism]]** — comprehensive cell-types/mechanisms/clinics review.

### 🔄 Drug repurposing

FDA-approved drugs from other indications used off-label for autism in stratified subgroups.

→ **[[topics/interventions/drug_repurposing/00_DRUG_REPURPOSING_OVERVIEW|Drug repurposing overview]]**

Includes: Rapamycin (mTOR), Metformin (AMPK + microbiome), Bumetanide (NKCC1/GABA switch), Suramin (antipurinergic — Naviaux), Memantine (NMDA), Riluzole (glutamate), Pioglitazone (PPAR-γ), Minocycline (microglial), Allopregnanolone (neurosteroid), low-dose Ivermectin (microbiome modulator — Hazan observation).

### 🧪 Peptides

Bioactive peptides — endogenous (oxytocin, vasopressin, IGF-1, mito-derived) or therapeutic (BPC-157, Selank, Semax, Cerebrolysin).

→ **[[topics/interventions/peptides/00_PEPTIDES_OVERVIEW|Peptides overview]]**

Includes: Intranasal oxytocin, vasopressin (AVP), IGF-1 (SHANK3-stratified — Hollander), exorphin/casomorphin biology (gut-derived opioid peptides — connects to GFCF diet), gut peptides (PYY, GLP-1, ghrelin), antimicrobial peptides (AMPs), mito-derived peptides (humanin, MOTS-c), BPC-157, Selank, Semax, Cerebrolysin.

### 🥗 Nutrition + lifestyle

For all autistic children — foundational layer regardless of subgroup.

→ **[[topics/interventions/nutrition_lifestyle/00_NUTRITION_OVERVIEW|Nutrition and lifestyle overview]]**

Includes: GFCF diet (gluten-free casein-free), Mediterranean diet, organic-first food choices, glyphosate avoidance, omega-3 / DHA, vitamin D, choline, sleep regulation, sensory environment, exercise, stress regulation.

## Universal principles across all interventions

Drawn from [[CLAUDE]] §1-§10 and the [[Hannah Poling framework]]:

1. **Stratify before treating.** Not every intervention works for every child. The biomarker workup (omics, immune, mitochondrial) tells you which interventions are likely to help.
2. **Effect sizes are subgroup-dependent.** Mixed RCT results almost always mean responder vs non-responder, not "doesn't work."
3. **Functional medicine operationalizes individual-level resolution.** Mainstream pediatrics gives population averages; functional medicine + the atlas frame each child as P(Φ | susceptibility, exposure).
4. **Re-test, iterate, adjust.** Most interventions work best when monitored with biomarkers and adjusted over time.
5. **Foundational layer first.** Diet + sleep + sensory environment + toxicant reduction before exotic interventions.
6. **Combinations matter.** Many interventions are additive or synergistic. The atlas's combinations.csv tracks this.
