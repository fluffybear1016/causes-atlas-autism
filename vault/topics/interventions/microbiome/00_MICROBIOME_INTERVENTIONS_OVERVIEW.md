# 🦠 Microbiome Interventions for Autism — Overview

The gut microbiome is the **single best-validated modifiable intervention layer for autism in 2026**. Mendelian randomization establishes formal causal pathways. Multi-omics integration shows mechanism. Intervention trials show effect. This is no longer fringe — this is rigorous evidence-based medicine that mainstream pediatrics is slow to deploy.

For parents: if your child has any GI symptoms, food sensitivities, eczema/allergies, history of antibiotics, or behavior changes that track with diet — this is the first intervention class to investigate.

## When to consider this class

Strong indication:
- GI symptoms (constipation, diarrhea, reflux, bloating, abdominal pain)
- Strong food sensitivities or food-allergy history
- Recurring rashes or eczema
- History of antibiotic exposure (multiple courses, especially in early life)
- Behavior changes that track with diet
- C-section birth (altered initial microbiome seeding)
- Formula-fed infancy (different microbiome than breastfed)
- History of GI infections, especially with persistent post-infection issues

Weaker indication / not first-line:
- Severe genetic syndromic autism without GI involvement
- Acute regression with primarily neuroinflammatory presentation (immune class first)

## The intervention options

### 1. Fecal microbiota transplantation (FMT)

The most aggressive and most evidence-supported microbiome intervention. Restores entire microbial community from a healthy donor.

**Two main protocols:**

**[[Hazan_Familial_FMT_Protocol]] — Sibling-donor familial FMT under FDA IND**
- [[Hazan_Sabine]] runs this at ProgenaBiome (California)
- Sibling-matched donor (because siblings share strain-level microbial ecology)
- Full pre/post sequencing
- Iterative reflorization over 6-12 months
- Documented dramatic outcomes including speech recovery in nonverbal children
- Cost: $5,000-$30,000 depending on protocol depth

**Adams MTT (Microbiota Transfer Therapy) — Arizona State University**
- [[Adams_James]] runs this in formal Phase 2 trials
- Peer-reviewed RCT-grade evidence (Kang 2017 PMID 28122648, Kang 2019 PMID 30967657)
- Structured oral preparation protocol
- 80% reduction in GI symptoms; behavioral improvements sustained at 2 years
- Trial enrollment is the gold-standard pathway

### 2. Targeted probiotic strains

Not all probiotics are equal. Strain selection matters substantially. Generic "Bifidobacterium" isn't equivalent to specific strain.

**Most-studied for autism:**
- *Bifidobacterium infantis* — particularly important for restoring depleted bifidobacterial population
- *Bifidobacterium longum* — broad SCFA producer
- *Bifidobacterium breve* — pediatric-relevant strain
- *Lactobacillus plantarum LP-W74* — anti-inflammatory
- *Lactobacillus reuteri DSM 17938* — selective use; not all kids tolerate (per [[Hazan_Sabine]])
- *Akkermansia muciniphila* — barrier-supporting, mucus-layer health
- *Faecalibacterium prausnitzii* — major butyrate producer

### 3. Prebiotics (fibers that feed beneficial bacteria)

- FOS (fructo-oligosaccharides)
- GOS (galacto-oligosaccharides) — particularly Bifidobacterium-supportive
- Inulin
- Partially-hydrolyzed guar fiber (PHGG)
- Resistant starch
- HMOs (human milk oligosaccharides) for infants

### 4. Postbiotics (microbial metabolites delivered directly)

- Butyrate (oral or rectal — research-grade)
- Sodium butyrate supplements
- Short-chain fatty acid blends

### 5. Targeted antimicrobial protocols

When the OAT or stool panel identifies overgrowth.

**Anti-Candida / antifungal:**
- Nystatin (oral, non-systemic)
- Caprylic acid
- Undecylenic acid
- Pau d'arco
- Biofilm disruptors (lumbrokinase, NAC, lactoferrin, EDTA combinations)

**Anti-Clostridia:**
- Vancomycin (oral, non-absorbable — for C. diff and related Clostridia)
- Metronidazole (caution; has neurological side effects)
- Saccharomyces boulardii (probiotic specifically targeting Clostridia and C. diff)

**Anti-parasite:**
- Artemisinin
- Berberine
- Black walnut
- Pumpkin seed (cucurbitin)

### 6. Diet as microbiome intervention

- Mediterranean / fiber-rich (general microbiome health)
- Specific carbohydrate diet (SCD) — for inflammatory bowel involvement
- GAPS diet — gut and psychology syndrome (Natasha Campbell-McBride)
- Low-FODMAP — for IBS-overlap symptoms
- Elimination diet (gluten, casein, soy, corn) per food sensitivity

See [[topics/interventions/nutrition_lifestyle/00_NUTRITION_OVERVIEW]] for diet detail.

## Workup before intervention

Before starting any major microbiome intervention, get baseline:

**Stool panels (pick one or two):**
- **Mosaic OAT (Organic Acids Test)** — urinary metabolite readout of microbial activity. ~$300. Includes arabinose (Candida), HPHPA (Clostridia), 4-cresol, plus mitochondrial markers. See [[Shaw_William]].
- **GI-MAP (Diagnostic Solutions)** — qPCR-based stool panel; bacteria + fungi + parasites + viruses + inflammation markers. ~$400.
- **Doctor's Data Comprehensive Stool Analysis** — combined panel.
- **Genova GI Effects** — combined panel.

**Adjunctive:**
- Plasma cytokine panel
- Tryptase (mast cell activation)
- Food sensitivity panel (IgG, not just IgE)

## Practical workflow

1. **Baseline workup** — stool panel + cytokine + sensitivities
2. **Address obvious overgrowth first** — antifungal/antimicrobial protocol if indicated
3. **Restore beneficial bacteria** — targeted strain probiotics + prebiotics
4. **Reassess at 8-12 weeks** — repeat stool panel, track behavior
5. **Consider FMT** if (a) no/insufficient response to gentler approaches, (b) child has major Bifidobacterium depletion, (c) family has resources for [[Hazan_Sabine]]'s clinic or [[Adams_James]]'s trial
6. **Maintain** — diet + ongoing low-dose probiotics + retesting every 3-6 months

## Bottom line

Microbiome interventions are the **highest-leverage, best-evidenced modifiable intervention class** in autism medicine in 2026. Effect sizes in stratified subgroups (especially Bifidobacterium-depleted, Clostridia-overgrown) are substantial. The workup is accessible. The interventions span from gentle (diet + probiotics) to aggressive (FMT under FDA IND).

If your child has any of the indication markers above, this is where to start.
