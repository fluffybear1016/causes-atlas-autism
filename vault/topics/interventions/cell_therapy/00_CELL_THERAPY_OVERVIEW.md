# 🧬 Cell Therapy for Autism — Overview

Cell therapy uses living cells (or their secreted products) as the therapeutic. For autism, this primarily means cord blood and umbilical cord mesenchymal stem cells (UC-MSCs). The mechanism is paracrine immune modulation + mitochondrial transfer + neurotrophic exosome delivery — NOT cell engraftment. Effects typically wane over 6-12 months without re-treatment.

This is one of the more underutilized but mechanistically defensible biomedical interventions in 2026. Phase 2 RCT evidence is real. Subgroup signal is real. Cost is significant.

## When to consider cell therapy

Strong indication:
- **Documented inflammatory phenotype** (elevated cytokines, GI inflammation)
- **Regressive course** (lost developmental skills after illness/vaccination/fever — [[Hannah Poling framework]])
- **Documented mitochondrial dysfunction** (per workup)
- **Family banked cord blood at birth** (autologous option available)
- **Plateau on first-line interventions** despite biomarker improvement

Weaker indication:
- Pure genetic syndromic autism (Fragile X, Rett, etc.) — different mechanism
- Strong anxiety presentation without inflammatory markers (Dawson 2020 subgroup analysis showed non-response in this profile)
- Not having tried first-line interventions yet

## Cell types used

**Autologous cord blood (own banked stem cells)**
- The child's own cells, banked at birth
- Zero rejection risk
- Most studied in [[Kurtzberg_Joanne]] / [[topics/omics/]] Duke trials
- Limited to families who banked

**Allogeneic umbilical cord blood (donor cord blood)**
- Healthy donor's cord blood
- Available even if family didn't bank
- Sun 2020 Duke Phase 1 established safety

**Allogeneic UC-MSC (umbilical cord mesenchymal stem cells)**
- MSCs isolated from umbilical cord matrix (Wharton's jelly)
- Immunologically privileged — don't trigger rejection
- Used in offshore Panama protocols ([[SRC-001454 31187597]] Riordan 2019)
- Used in Chinese hospital trials

**Bone marrow MSC**
- Harvested from autologous bone marrow
- Lower-tier evidence for autism
- Sometimes given intrathecally (Sharma 2013)

**MSC-derived exosomes (emerging)**
- Secreted vesicles from MSCs containing growth factors + miRNAs
- Some clinics offer as alternative to whole-cell therapy
- Mechanistically reasonable; minimal autism-specific peer-reviewed evidence

## Mechanism of action

Paracrine and not via engraftment. Five overlapping mechanisms:

1. **Immune modulation** — MSCs suppress activated T cells, induce Tregs, shift M1→M2 macrophages, reduce TNF-α/IL-1β/IL-6
2. **Microglial activation reduction** — paracrine signaling crosses BBB or signals via peripheral immune trafficking; reduces chronic neuroinflammation
3. **Mitochondrial transfer** — MSCs transfer functional mitochondria to neighboring stressed cells via tunneling nanotubes and extracellular vesicles
4. **Exosome-delivered neurotrophic factors** — BDNF, GDNF, regulatory miRNAs persist in recipient even after cells are cleared
5. **Blood-brain barrier integrity** — paracrine support of BBB repair

## Where to actually pursue this

### Tier 1 — Formal trials under FDA IND (gold standard)

**Duke Marcus Center for Cellular Cures**
- [[Kurtzberg_Joanne]] (cell therapy lead) + [[Dawson_Geraldine]] (autism research lead)
- Multiple ongoing trials
- Some require autologous (banked) cord blood; some accept allogeneic
- Trial registration: ClinicalTrials.gov "Duke autism cord blood"
- Cost: typically free to participants when enrolled (research-funded)

### Tier 2 — Established overseas clinics with peer-reviewed data

**Stem Cell Institute Panama**
- [[Riordan_Neil]] founder
- Most-published offshore UC-MSC autism program
- Multiple infusions of allogeneic UC-MSC
- Cost: $15,000-$30,000 typical full protocol

**Mayo Clinic / select academic centers**
- Occasional expanded-access protocols
- Case-by-case basis

**Chinese hospital programs**
- Multiple published trials (Lv 2013 [[SRC-001453 23978163]] and others)
- Variable methodological quality
- Generally higher cell doses than Western protocols

### Tier 3 — Avoid

**US "stem cell clinics" not under IND**
- Most use adipose-derived stromal vascular fraction or amniotic-fluid products
- No autism-specific evidence base
- FDA has issued warnings to many
- Wide cost variation, often $5,000-$15,000 per infusion of dubious quality

**Anywhere making "cure" claims**
- Legitimate clinical literature does not support cure claims
- Anyone making them is selling

## Pre-treatment workup

Essential before considering this intervention:

**Inflammatory profile:**
- Cytokine panel (IL-6, TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-17)
- CRP, ESR
- Complement (C3, C4)

**Mitochondrial markers:**
- Lactate, pyruvate, L:P ratio
- Acylcarnitine profile
- Organic acids

**Immune workup:**
- ANA, RF, thyroid antibodies, gluten antibodies
- Cunningham Panel if PANS features
- Total immunoglobulins

**Genetic context:**
- CMA + Fragile X + autism gene panel if not already done

A reasonable cytokine and mitochondrial workup before cell therapy is the difference between an evidence-aligned intervention and an expensive shot in the dark.

## Cost reality

- **Duke trials:** Typically free to participants
- **Stem Cell Institute Panama:** $15,000-$30,000 per protocol (multiple infusions)
- **Chinese hospital programs:** $10,000-$25,000
- **US offshore-style clinics:** $5,000-$15,000 per infusion (quality varies)
- **Cord blood banking at birth (relevant for future):** $1,500-$2,500 collection + $100-$250/year storage

## Bottom line

Cell therapy is a real, mechanistically grounded, peer-reviewed-trial-supported intervention for the inflammatory + non-anxious + cognitively-able subgroup of autistic children, particularly those with regressive history. It is not a cure. It is not first-line. Effects wane over 6-12 months.

For children fitting the responder profile who have plateaued on first-line interventions and have family resources — Duke trial enrollment is the gold standard, Stem Cell Institute Panama is the most-published commercial second tier.
