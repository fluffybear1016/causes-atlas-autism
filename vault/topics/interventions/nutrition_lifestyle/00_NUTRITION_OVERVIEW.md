# 🥗 Nutrition + Lifestyle Interventions for Autism — Overview

This is the **foundational layer** that every autistic child benefits from regardless of subgroup. Diet, sleep, sensory environment, exercise, stress regulation, and toxicant reduction set the baseline on which every other intervention works. Done well, these alone can produce meaningful improvement. Done poorly, they undermine even the most aggressive biomedical protocols.

## Why this is foundational

Per [[Hyman_Mark]]'s functional medicine framework: most chronic-disease pathology in pediatrics begins with nutritional, environmental, and lifestyle factors that compound over years. For autism specifically:

- **Diet shapes the microbiome** ([[topics/interventions/microbiome/00_MICROBIOME_INTERVENTIONS_OVERVIEW]])
- **Methyl donor intake gates the methylation cycle** ([[topics/interventions/methylation_pathway/00_METHYLATION_OVERVIEW]])
- **Antioxidant intake supports glutathione + mitochondrial function** ([[topics/interventions/mitochondrial/00_MITOCHONDRIAL_OVERVIEW]])
- **Toxicant exposure load drives inflammation + epigenetic dysregulation** ([[topics/omics/Epigenetics_of_Autism]])
- **Sleep regulates immune and methylation cycles**
- **Sensory environment affects nervous system regulation directly**

You cannot fix any of those at the biomedical layer if the foundational layer is broken.

## The interventions

### 1. Dietary protocols

**[[topics/interventions/nutrition_lifestyle/GFCF_Diet|Gluten-free / casein-free (GFCF) diet]]** ([[INT-0041]] — csrs 51.5)
- Eliminates gluten and dairy
- Mechanism: opioid peptide (gluteomorphin/casomorphin) reduction + intestinal permeability improvement + immune calming
- Best evidence: zonulin-elevated / GI-symptomatic subgroup ([[CLAUDE]] §9 effect heterogeneity)
- Trial duration: 6-12 weeks for response assessment
- Researcher: Karl Reichelt, Paul Shattock historical; broader functional medicine community

**Specific Carbohydrate Diet (SCD)**
- Eliminates complex carbohydrates that feed dysbiotic bacteria
- Use case: IBD-overlap, severe Clostridia overgrowth
- Originating from Elaine Gottschall's *Breaking the Vicious Cycle*

**GAPS diet** (Gut and Psychology Syndrome — Natasha Campbell-McBride)
- Phased gut-healing protocol
- Eliminates fermented and starch-heavy foods initially
- Reintroduces selectively

**Low-FODMAP diet**
- For IBS-overlap symptoms specifically
- Time-limited (2-6 weeks elimination, then reintroduction)

**Mediterranean diet**
- Foundational anti-inflammatory framework
- High vegetable, fish, olive oil, modest grains
- Most-evidenced general health diet

**Ketogenic diet (medical, supervised)**
- For epileptic ASD specifically (clear FDA-approved indication)
- For mitochondrial dysfunction (some response)
- Not for general autism without specific indication
- Requires nutritionist + neurologist supervision

**Elimination diet (broader)**
- Beyond GFCF: also remove soy, corn, eggs, nuts as indicated by sensitivity testing
- Systematic add-back to identify triggers

### 2. Specific nutrients (foundational)

**Omega-3 / DHA** ([[INT-related fish oil]])
- Foundational for neurodevelopment
- Dose: 500-2000 mg combined EPA+DHA daily
- Form: triglyceride or phospholipid, not ethyl ester for kids
- High-quality, third-party-tested only

**Vitamin D3** ([[INT-0013]] — csrs 67.6)
- Deficient in vast majority of children, especially with limited sun
- Dose: 1000-2000 IU/day (children); 2000-5000 IU/day (older); calibrated by 25-OH-D level
- Target: 50-80 ng/mL
- Critical for immune regulation, methylation, gene expression

**Choline** ([[INT-0030]] — csrs 63.7)
- Massively under-consumed; most prenatal vitamins inadequate
- Recommended pregnancy: >450 mg/day (most prenatals provide ~50 mg)
- Dietary: eggs (most concentrated source), liver, fish
- Supplement: alpha-GPC, CDP-choline (citicoline), phosphatidylcholine
- Affects methylation cycle + brain development

**Methylated B-vitamins**
- Methyl-B12, methylated folate (5-MTHF), B6/P5P, B2/R5P
- See [[topics/interventions/methylation_pathway/00_METHYLATION_OVERVIEW]]

**Magnesium** (Mg-glycinate, Mg-malate, Mg-threonate for BBB-crossing)
- Cofactor in 300+ enzyme reactions
- Calming, sleep-supporting

**Zinc**
- Often deficient especially in pyrolurics
- Dose: 10-30 mg/day (children); calibrated to plasma zinc + serum copper

### 3. Foundational lifestyle

**Sleep regulation**
- Adequate sleep is when most epigenetic + immune + methylation maintenance happens
- Children need 9-12 hours depending on age
- Melatonin (0.3-3 mg) for sleep-onset issues
- Sleep environment: cool, dark, no screens 1+ hours before bed
- Circadian rhythm support: morning sunlight, evening dim light

**Exercise** ([[INT-0047]] — csrs 67.2)
- Aerobic exercise: 30-60 min daily
- Resistance training: 2-3x weekly age-appropriate
- Improves mitochondrial biogenesis, BDNF, mood, sleep, microbiome
- Outdoor preferred (sun + nature exposure)

**Sensory environment**
- Low-stimulus environment helps sensory-overwhelmed kids
- Noise reduction, light reduction in evening, fabric texture awareness
- OT-led sensory diet may help

**Stress regulation**
- Chronic stress elevates cortisol → mast cell activation → neuroinflammation
- Mindfulness, age-appropriate emotional regulation tools
- Parent stress regulation (downstream effect on child)

**Outdoor time + sun exposure**
- Vitamin D synthesis
- Circadian regulation
- Microbiome diversification (soil exposure)
- Nature has measurable nervous-system regulating effects

### 4. Toxicant reduction

**Glyphosate avoidance** (per [[Seneff_Stephanie]])
- Most-used agrochemical; ubiquitous in conventional grains, oats, soy, sugar
- Buy organic for: wheat, oats, corn, soy, sugar
- Glyphosate disrupts methylation cycle, microbiome (shikimate pathway), sulfate metabolism

**Pesticide reduction**
- "Dirty Dozen" organic priorities (EWG list)
- Organic produce for high-residue items

**Plastic reduction**
- BPA, phthalates, microplastics
- Glass + stainless steel + ceramic for food storage
- Avoid plastic water bottles in heat (leaching)

**Air quality**
- HEPA filtration
- Avoid wildfire smoke, urban pollution where possible
- Cooking ventilation (reduce indoor combustion exposure)

**Water quality**
- Filtered drinking water (carbon block + reverse osmosis for full filtration)
- Avoid fluoride if possible (some autism subgroups sensitive)
- Test home water for heavy metals if older home

**Heavy metal reduction**
- Mercury: limit high-mercury fish (tuna, swordfish), choose low-mercury fish (sardines, salmon, mackerel)
- Aluminum: avoid aluminum cookware, antiperspirant, certain medications when possible
- Lead: test home for lead paint if pre-1978 home

**EMF awareness**
- Distance from devices, especially during sleep
- Hard-wire instead of WiFi where possible (especially in bedroom)
- Speculative-tier evidence; low-cost intervention

## Practical workflow

For a newly-diagnosed child:

**Week 1-2:** Optimize foundational layer
- Increase organic vegetables and fruit (especially berries, leafy greens, broccoli sprouts)
- Reduce processed food, sugar, refined seed oils
- Establish sleep routine (consistent bedtime, dim lighting in evening)
- Daily outdoor + sunlight exposure
- Quality omega-3 + vitamin D3 + multivitamin (third-party-tested)

**Week 3-4:** Begin trial protocols based on subgroup
- GFCF trial if GI / food sensitivity / behavioral indicators (6-12 weeks)
- Probiotic + prebiotic if no contraindication
- Choline if not getting from diet (eggs)

**Month 2-3:** Workup integration
- Mosaic OAT + GI panel
- Methylation cycle workup
- Mitochondrial markers

**Month 3+:** Intervention layering
- Layer biomedical interventions (methylation support, mito cocktail, immune modulation as indicated) on top of foundational nutrition + lifestyle
- Maintain foundational layer indefinitely

## Bottom line

Nutrition and lifestyle are not the exciting interventions. They're the *foundational* interventions. No biomedical protocol works as well in a child eating processed food, sleeping poorly, exposed to toxicants, and carrying chronic stress.

Get the foundational layer right first. Then layer biomedical interventions on top. Most parents who think their child "didn't respond" to a biomedical protocol find substantially better response after 8-12 weeks of foundational nutrition + lifestyle optimization.

This is also the most accessible class — diet and lifestyle don't require specialist clinicians, expensive panels, or prescriptions. Start here while the workup for other classes is in progress.
