# 🧪 Biomarkers — Master Index

The biomarker layer is the **operational bridge** between the atlas's other entities. Every test you can order, every measurable variable that informs intervention choice, lives here.

**164 biomarkers across 27 categories**, each with: sample type, reference range, clinical significance, atlas-entity wikilinks (mechanisms it indicates, phenotypes it stratifies, interventions it modulates, hypotheses it tests), test logistics (lab, cost, CLIA status, turnaround), SNP-conditional interpretation where relevant.

For the auto-generated alphabetical catalog see [[BIOMARKERS_INDEX|biomarkers/INDEX]] (one page per BIO-NNNN entry).

## 📚 The 27 categories

### Foundational metabolic / methylation
- [[Methylation_Panel_Overview]] *(deep-dive pending)* — SAM/SAH, methionine, homocysteine, B12 (MMA + holoTC), folate (plasma + RBC), B6/P5P, whole-blood histamine. **14 biomarkers.** The single most clinically-actionable panel for ASD.

## 🧭 Decision tree — what to test first

Match clinical picture to category:

| Clinical picture | Order this panel first |
|---|---|
| Language delay / regression | [[Folate_Receptor_Panel]] + [[Methylation_Panel_Overview]] |
| GI symptoms / food sensitivity | [[OAT_Microbial_Markers]] + [[GI_Barrier_Panel]] |
| Regression after illness/fever | [[Mitochondrial_Panel]] + [[Heat_Shock_Panel]] |

## 🧬 What's NOT here

These are biomarker categories the atlas explicitly does not cover or under-covers:

- **Clinical pediatric assessment scales** (CARS, ATEC, ADOS, M-CHAT) — these are clinical tools, not lab biomarkers.
- **Behavioral / cognitive testing** (IQ, EF, language assessments) — same reason.
- **Wearable biomarkers** beyond HRV — emerging field; not yet clinically standardized.
- **Multi-omics composite scores** — addressed in [[topics/omics/Multi_Omics_Causal_Pathways_in_Autism]] rather than here.

## Bottom line

This is the operational layer. A clinician with a child's lab results can land on any biomarker page, see what mechanism it indicates, what phenotype it stratifies, what intervention it predicts, and what hypothesis it tests — all wikilinked to the deeper atlas content.

For the parent reader: biomarkers are how you go from "my child has these symptoms" to "here are the specific tests to confirm and the specific interventions that should respond."

For the researcher: biomarkers are the empirical anchor that prevents the atlas from being purely theoretical. Every hypothesis that has a measurable biomarker is testable. Every intervention that modulates a biomarker is monitorable.
