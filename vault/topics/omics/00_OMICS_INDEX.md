# 🔬 Omics — The Molecular Biology of Autism

This section explains *what's actually happening at the molecular level* in autism. Each "omics" is a different layer of biology you can measure: DNA, RNA, proteins, metabolites, microbiome, etc.

**For parents:** You don't need to read all 8 pages. Most useful starter pages are [[Metabolomics_in_Autism]] (clinically actionable today) and [[Microbiomics_and_Gut_Brain_Axis]] (microbiome interventions). Then [[Multi_Omics_Integration_for_Autism]] if you want the big picture.

**For clinicians:** Start with [[Multi_Omics_Integration_for_Autism]], then drill into the specific layer that maps to your patient's clinical picture.

**For researchers:** [[Multi_Omics_Causal_Pathways_in_Autism]] is the rigorous causal-inference review.

### Individual omics layers

- **[[Genomics_of_Autism]]** — DNA layer. Two-architecture model (rare large-effect + polygenic). SFARI tier system. Key gene families (SHANK, mTOR, FMR1, methylation-cycle, mitochondrial). Clinical genetic testing. Pharmacogenomics. *Most important if your child has multi-organ involvement or syndromic features.*

- **[[Epigenetics_of_Autism]]** — The modifiable molecular layer. DNA methylation in autism. Maternal stress + immune + toxicant + nutritional effects on fetal methylation. Paternal age and preconception optimization. Transgenerational inheritance. *Most important for prevention if you're planning future pregnancies.*

- **[[Transcriptomics_in_Autism]]** — RNA layer. Bulk + single-cell + iPSC + spatial. Three convergent gene-expression modules in ASD brain (synaptic↓, immune↑, chromatin dysregulated). Cell-type resolution revolution. *Most important for severe / treatment-resistant cases where personalized neuron screening might apply.*

- **[[Proteomics_in_Autism]]** — Protein + post-translational modification layer. Synaptic scaffolding deficits. mTOR phosphoproteomics. Cytokine + Cunningham + mast cell panels as accessible clinical proteomics. *Most important for inflammatory / mast cell / PANS subgroups.*

- **[[Metabolomics_in_Autism]]** — **The clinically most-actionable omics layer in 2026.** Methylation cycle, mitochondrial, microbial, neurotransmitter, oxidative stress signatures. The Mosaic OAT framework. *Most important — start here if you're new.*

- **[[Microbiomics_and_Gut_Brain_Axis]]** — Gut microbiome composition + strain genetics + metaproteomics + metabolite production. Causal-pathway evidence (Mendelian randomization + multi-omics chains). Clinical FMT/MTT/probiotic landscape. *Most important if your child has GI symptoms or behavioral sensitivity to diet.*

## How these layers connect

```
        DNA ──→ RNA ──→ Protein ──→ Metabolite ──→ Phenotype
                                  ↑              ↑
                       Epigenetic regulation  Microbiome
                                              modifies
```

Each layer modulates the next. Multi-omics integration combines them all. The causal anchor is genetic + epigenetic; the modifiable intervention layers are metabolomics + microbiomics + immune.
