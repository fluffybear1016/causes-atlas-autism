> Tuberous sclerosis complex (TSC) is a rare genetic disease caused by heterozygous loss-of-function variants in TSC1 or TSC2. Patients present with benign tumours known as hamartomas in the brain, eyes, lungs, kidneys, heart and skin. Many hamartomas contain mosaic second hit variants in TSC1 or TSC2. The most disabling features of TSC include epilepsy and TSC-associated neuropsychiatric disorders (TAND) such as intellectual disability and autism spectrum disorder.
