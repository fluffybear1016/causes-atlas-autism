## Evidence fragments


### EVD-001425 — result (positive)


> Huang Q et al. (2026, Commun Biol, PMID 41580462): randomized double-blind crossover study (n=24 non-autistic + 15 autistic, 93 visits) of arbaclofen (GABA-B agonist) vs placebo using phase-amplitude coupling (PAC) of resting-state EEG. (1) Autistic participants exhibited significantly higher theta-beta PAC, especially in limbic network. (2) High-dose arbaclofen (30mg) shifted PAC in visual/somatomotor networks toward non-autistic levels. (3) Limbic PAC NORMALIZED by low-dose (15mg) but REEMERGED at high-dose — paradoxical dose response. Conclusion: 'altered GABAergic responsivity in autism, helping explain... paradoxical reactions and dose sensitivity.'


**Supports:** hypothesis: [[HYP-0071 Brainstem pons hypoplasia + GABA developmental switch failure]]; mechanism: [[MEC-0007 GABA glutamate imbalance]]
