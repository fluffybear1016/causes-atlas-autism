# Commercial licensing — Causes Atlas

The Causes Atlas is **free** for any noncommercial use — research,
personal study, education, charities, public-health and government
institutions, hobby and amateur work — under the PolyForm Noncommercial
License 1.0.0 (code) and CC BY-NC 4.0 (science and data). Use it, study
it, modify it, share it. Just credit the project.

**If you monetize it, you need a commercial license.**

## What counts as commercial use

You need a paid commercial license if you use the Causes Atlas — the
code, the scoring/ingestion engines, the vault, the datasets, the
knowledge graph, or works derived from them — in any way that is part of
generating revenue. That includes, without limitation:

  - Building a paid product, app, or service on top of it
  - Offering paid clinical, diagnostic, or consulting services that use it
  - Selling access, reports, or analyses derived from it
  - Incorporating it into proprietary or for-profit software
  - Internal use by a for-profit company in service of its business
    (note: charitable organizations, educational institutions, public
    research organizations, public-safety/health organizations,
    environmental organizations, and government institutions are
    **noncommercial** under these licenses regardless of funding)

If you are not sure whether your use is commercial, ask before you ship.

## How to license commercially

Commercial licenses are granted by the Causes Atlas project on a
case-by-case basis. Terms are reasonable and scaled to use.

To request a commercial license, open a GitHub issue titled
`Commercial license request` describing:

  1. Who you are (individual or organization)
  2. What you want to build or do
  3. Expected scale (users, revenue model, deployment)

A maintainer will follow up to arrange terms.

## Why it works this way

The science stays free so that families, clinicians, and researchers can
use it without a paywall — that is the point of the project. The
commercial license exists so that anyone building a business on this
work contributes back, and that contribution funds further research.
Free to use. Credited. Paid if you monetize.
